# Pixie — Go-to-Market Strategy

## GTM Philosophy

Pixie solves a two-sided cold start problem: no agents without tasks, no tasks without agents. The launch strategy resolves this by building agent density 6 weeks before the first customer task goes live. Every city launch follows this sequence — supply first, then demand.

---

## Beachhead Market

**Use case**: Competitor store checks + own-brand shelf audits for CPG brands
**Why this beachhead**:
- Lowest regulatory friction (publicly displayed products, no privacy concerns)
- Existing budget in CPG companies for competitive intelligence
- Short sales cycle (Brand Manager can approve $5–$50K without VP sign-off)
- High frequency = recurring revenue from Month 1
- Proven willingness to pay (Field Agent charges $30–$175/task and has 2,000+ brands)

**Avoid for Year 1**: Insurance (complex validation requirements, 6–18 month procurement), Government (slow procurement), Illinois (BIPA risk)

## Sales Motion

### Stage 1: Founder-Led Sales (Months 0–12)

Founders sell the first 25 customers. No sales hires yet.

**Outreach channels**:
1. Warm network: CPG brand managers in founders' network
2. LinkedIn direct outreach: search "VP Category Management + [CPG brand]"
3. Conference presence: Expo West (March), NACS (October), IRI Summit
4. Content marketing: LinkedIn posts about CPG out-of-stock cost studies

**Outreach message**:
> "Hi [Name], I saw you're at [Brand]. We're building on-demand competitive shelf intelligence — real photos from any store, AI-extracted, delivered in hours, not weeks. Would 50 free tasks be worth 20 minutes of your time?"

**Goal**: 3 meetings/week, 1 pilot started/week, 40% pilot-to-paid conversion

### Stage 2: Sales Team (Months 9–18)

Hire 1 Account Executive at Month 9, second AE at Month 15.

**AE profile**: 3+ years CPG SaaS sales (NielsenIQ, Numerator, IRI, Intouch Insight alumni preferred). Existing book of CPG contacts. OTE: $120–$150K (50/50 base/commission).

**Sales cycle target**: 30 days for Growth plan, 60–90 days for Enterprise

## Pilot Structure

The Pixie pilot is the primary conversion mechanism. Every enterprise relationship starts here.

### Free Pilot (for prospects with no budget yet)
- Duration: 30 days
- Scope: 50 tasks in 1–2 locations
- Deliverable: Full AI-extracted report + ROI analysis
- Goal: Prove "this is better than what you do today"

### Paid Starter Pack ($500 credit)
- Preferred over free pilot (skin-in-game → higher engagement)
- 30–50 tasks depending on task type
- Same deliverable
- Expenseable without procurement approval at most companies

### Pilot Success Criteria (agreed upfront with customer)
At pilot kickoff, define: "We'll call this a success if [specific outcome]."
Examples:
- "We find at least 1 out-of-stock situation you didn't know about"
- "We get competitive pricing data from 50 stores in 48 hours"
- "AI output matches your own team's manual checks with >80% accuracy"

**Conversion trigger**: At Day 28, present ROI analysis:
- Tasks completed, fulfillment rate, time to delivery
- What the AI found that they didn't know
- $ value of discoveries (OOS events, compliance gaps, price changes)
- Growth subscription proposal with annual pricing

**Target conversion rate**: 60% of pilots → paid within 30 days of completion

## City Launch Playbook

Every city launch follows this exact sequence.

### 6 Weeks Before Launch: Agent Recruitment

**City Captain hired** (local contractor, $2,000/month, 20 hrs/week)
- Responsibilities: poster campaigns, local community outreach, agent onboarding support
- Ideal profile: local gig worker community organizer, social media presence, knows the city

**Agent recruitment channels**:
1. Targeted Facebook/Instagram ads: "Earn up to $35/hour photographing your city"
   - Targeting: 18–45, gig worker interests (Uber/DoorDash/TaskRabbit users), local geography
   - Budget: $5,000/city ($10–20 CPM, target 2,000 installs → 400 verified agents)
2. Reddit: r/[city], r/Entrepreneur, gig worker subreddits
3. University flyers: 3–5 universities per city, target student unions + career centers
4. Craigslist: "Gig work" and "Creative" sections
5. Existing gig worker Slack/Discord communities

**Recruitment threshold before launch**: 200 verified, ID-checked agents

### Launch Week: First Customer Tasks

- Max 20 tasks on Day 1 (test the system with controlled volume)
- Monitor: fulfillment rate, time-to-completion, agent rejection reasons, AI validation pass rate
- Human review of first 50 submissions (quality calibration)
- Escalate any issues before scaling task volume

**City health metrics** (checked weekly):
- Fulfillment rate: % tasks completed within 2-hour SLA
- Agent retention: % of agents completing tasks in 2nd consecutive week
- Customer acceptance rate: % AI output accepted without dispute
- Green = Fulfillment ≥80%, Amber = 60–79%, Red = <60%

**Kill switch**: If fulfillment rate <50% by Week 8 → pause customer tasks, 2 more weeks agent recruitment

### City Success Criteria (by Week 12)
- 500+ active agents
- >80% task fulfillment rate within 2-hour SLA
- 2+ paying customers in city
- First customer has requested expansion (more stores, more tasks)

### City Selection Criteria
1. CPG brand presence (# of major retail stores per sq mile)
2. Gig worker density (existing Uber/DoorDash driver count)
3. University population (ambassador program fuel)
4. Regulatory complexity (low = better for Year 1)
5. No direct competitor dominance

## Agent Acquisition Strategy

### Channel Mix + CAC Targets
| Channel | Year 1 Target % | CAC Target |
|---|---|---|
| Ambassador program (campus/community) | 40% | $10–$20 |
| Agent referral program | 30% | $15–$25 |
| Paid social (FB/IG/TikTok) | 20% | $30–$60 |
| Organic (app store, SEO) | 10% | $5–$15 |
| **Blended CAC** | 100% | **$20–$25** |

### Campus Ambassador Program
- Role: Student earns $200/month (in Pixie credits) for recruiting 10+ active agents from campus
- Ideal ambassador: club president, athlete, social media micro-influencer, existing gig worker
- Package: branded t-shirt, QR code flyers, social media kit, referral tracking link
- Progression: Top ambassadors → City Captain ($500/month)
- Scale: 50 ambassadors → 500 active agents/month at $10K/month cost

### Agent-to-Agent Referral
- Mechanism: Agent A shares code → Agent B signs up → Agent B completes 10 tasks → Agent A earns $25
- Agent B receives: $15 welcome bonus on first task
- Friction: Share button on earnings screen ("I just made $47 in 2 hours — join me")
- Auto-generated earnings card for social sharing (Instagram-optimized design)

## Customer Acquisition Strategy

### Content + SEO
Target keywords: "shelf audit software," "competitive intelligence retail," "mystery shopping alternative," "real-time shelf data"
Content: "The Hidden Cost of Out-of-Stocks" (targets CPG Brand Managers), "Why Your Field Team Can't Cover All Your Stores" (targets VPs)

### Conference Strategy
Year 1 conferences:
- **Expo West** (Anaheim, March) — Natural food CPG brands, very high density of target ICP
- **NACS Show** (October) — Convenience retail + CPG
- **GroceryShop** (September) — Retail tech forward-looking event
- **IRI/Circana Summit** — Data buyers

Tactic: Sponsor networking lunch at one conference, demo live shelf data from show floor ("We just captured shelf data from 10 stores within a mile of this convention center").

### Partnership Sales
**Market research agency partnerships**: IRI, Kantar, Nielsen partners who could include Pixie data in their syndicated reports. This is the fastest path to scale — one partnership = access to hundreds of CPG customers.

**Approach**: "White-label Pixie data within your existing reports" — we provide the data, they provide the customer relationship. Revenue share: 30% to agency.
