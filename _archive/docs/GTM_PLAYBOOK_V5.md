## Cycle 171: First 90 Days Sales Playbook
*Week-by-week for the CPG enterprise sale*

### The Sales Motion

Mosaic's enterprise sales is a founder-led, consultative motion for the first 24 months. No SDRs, no marketing-generated inbound pipeline (initially). The founders know the product, know the customer's pain (they've lived it through the research cycles), and can close a $60K–$300K pilot-to-annual deal without a sales deck review cycle.

The sale always flows through the same 13-week structure. Compress it or extend it — but don't skip steps. The steps exist to systematically de-risk the decision for the buyer and surface objections early before they kill the deal late.

---

### Weeks 1–3: Discovery + Stakeholder Mapping

**Week 1: Initial contact + ICP qualification**

Outreach approach (for now, personal network + warm intro):
- Message to first-degree LinkedIn connections in CPG field sales, category management, shopper marketing: "I'm building a tool for field intelligence — 30-min call to stress-test an idea?"
- Target: VP of Sales / National Field Sales Manager / Director of Category Management
- Qualification criteria (must have 3 of 4): ① has distributed products in ≥500 retail locations ② currently spends >$100K/year on field intelligence ③ has experienced a compliance or OOS failure in the last 12 months ④ is in CPG (food, bev, beauty, household, pet)

**Week 1 deliverable**: 3 discovery calls booked.

**Week 2: Discovery calls**

Goal of discovery: understand the specific compliance/OOS/pricing failure they experienced. Do not pitch. Ask:
1. "Walk me through how you currently verify shelf compliance at your top retail chain."
2. "What was the most expensive field intelligence failure you had in the last 12 months?"
3. "If you could see any 100 stores in the country right now — what would you check first?"
4. "What would it be worth to you if you could get that answer in 4 hours?"

By the end of a 45-minute call, you should know:
- The specific dollar value of the pain (they will usually quantify it — $300K OOS event, $170K missed promotional placement)
- Who owns the budget (often not the person you're talking to — get the name of the economic buyer)
- Whether they have existing contracts with Field Agent, BeMyEye, or a mystery shopping firm (understand what switching costs look like)

**Week 2 deliverable**: Completed discovery note for each call. 2 of 3 calls qualify for pilot scoping.

**Week 3: Stakeholder mapping + champion development**

The champion (the person you talked to in Week 2) needs ammunition to take the pilot to the economic buyer (VP of Sales or CFO). Give them that ammunition.

Actions:
- Send them a 1-page ROI calculator customized to their numbers: "Based on what you told me — 4,200 stores, 19% current coverage, $170K missed promotional placement — here's what Mosaic looks like for you."
- Offer to be on the call when they present internally: "I can join as a 10-minute subject matter expert — happy to handle the technical questions."
- Send a 1-slide competitive comparison: "What you're currently paying vs. what Mosaic costs for the same coverage. No integration required, 30-day pilot."

**Week 3 deliverable**: Champion has internal buy-in or you know the objection that needs addressing.

---

### Weeks 4–6: Pilot Scoping + Contract

**Week 4: Pilot scoping call**

With champion + economic buyer (target: both on the call).

Agenda (30 minutes):
1. Confirm the specific use case (shelf compliance, OOS detection, pricing, or combination)
2. Define pilot scope: how many stores, what SKUs, which retailer, what SLA
3. Define success criteria: what specific outcome makes this pilot a success?
4. Review pilot pricing ($5K–$15K depending on scope)
5. Timeline: "We can have you up and running within 10 business days of contract signature."

**Non-negotiable in scoping**: Success criteria must be agreed in writing before the pilot starts. Vague success criteria — "we'll know it when we see it" — always result in disputed outcomes at pilot conclusion.

Recommended success criteria language:
- "Fulfillment rate ≥80% of stores within 4-hour SLA"
- "AI extraction accuracy ≥90% on in-stock/OOS determination (validated against spot-check)"
- "Compliance findings actionable for at least one distributor follow-up conversation"

**Week 4 deliverable**: Scoping document agreed, pilot pricing agreed verbally.

**Week 5: Pilot contract execution**

Send the pilot agreement (see Cycle 172 for template). Target 3–5 business days to signature.

Common contract objections and responses:
- "Legal needs to review" → "Understood. We've kept the contract to 4 pages specifically to minimize legal turnaround. Happy to get on a call with your legal team to walk through any points directly."
- "We need to add our standard vendor terms" → Accept most standard vendor terms. The only non-negotiable: IP ownership of the collected data remains with Mosaic (customer gets a license to use the results, not ownership of the raw data and AI models). This is the model.
- "Can we reduce the pilot fee?" → Offer to scope down (fewer stores) rather than reduce per-store price. Pricing integrity matters.

**Week 5 deliverable**: Signed pilot agreement.

**Week 6: Pilot setup**

- Store list upload + verification (fix address errors, confirm store access requirements)
- Planogram + SKU brief uploaded to enterprise portal
- Success criteria entered into the portal (visible to both sides throughout pilot)
- Collector pool availability check: confirm Mosaic has ≥5 verified collectors per store location (flag any gaps >3 days before launch)
- Customer onboarding call: 30-minute portal walkthrough, introduce customer to their account manager

**Week 6 deliverable**: Pilot live, first tasks posted.

---

### Weeks 7–10: Pilot Execution

**Week 7–8: Active monitoring + early wins**

The single most important activity in the pilot is surfacing a memorable ROI moment as early as possible.

Within the first 48 hours of data coming in, look for:
- Any OOS finding that the customer's existing tools haven't flagged
- Any compliance failure that contradicts the distributor's last report
- Any competitive pricing anomaly that's actionable in the next 48 hours

When you find one, call the champion immediately: "We've been live for 36 hours and we've already found something. Store #47 in the Leeds cluster shows your main SKU out of stock — the distributor's report from Monday said it was fine. I'm sending you the photos now."

This is the close. Everything after this is logistics.

**Week 9: Mid-pilot check-in**

30-minute call with champion + economic buyer:
- Review fulfillment rate, AI accuracy stats, compliance score distribution
- Walk through the most interesting findings from weeks 7–8
- Preview annual contract: "Based on what we're seeing, here's what an annual program would look like for your top 2 retailers."

**Week 9 deliverable**: Annual contract proposal sent to champion.

**Week 10: Final pilot report delivery**

Mosaic-generated executive summary:
- Fulfillment statistics
- Key findings (compliance issues discovered, OOS events detected, pricing anomalies)
- Estimated ROI: "In 30 days, Mosaic identified [X] compliance failures and [Y] OOS events. Based on your numbers, the estimated value of rapid detection: $[Z]."
- Annual contract recommendation with three tiers (Growth / Professional / Enterprise)

---

### Weeks 11–13: Results QBR + Annual Contract Close

**Week 11: Pilot QBR (Quarterly Business Review)**

In-person or video call with champion + economic buyer + any additional stakeholders they've added.

Structure:
1. Pilot results (10 min) — led by Mosaic
2. Customer reaction — open discussion
3. ROI validation — walk through the ROI calculator with their actual numbers
4. Annual program options (10 min) — present three tiers, recommend middle tier
5. Q&A

**The ask**: "Based on what we've seen, we'd recommend the Professional tier at $8,000/month. That gives you 400 store checks per month, 4-hour SLA, API access, and your dedicated account manager. Ready to move forward?"

**Week 12: Contract negotiation**

Most common enterprise negotiation points:
- Annual vs. monthly billing: offer 10% discount for annual prepay
- SLA commitments: tighten SLA language to be specific per tier (never agree to "best efforts")
- Data ownership: maintain Mosaic IP ownership on the extraction models and the raw data. Customer owns their results data.
- Pilot fee credit: offer to credit the pilot fee against the first month of the annual contract. Makes the decision feel free.

**Week 13: Annual contract signed**

Target: 100% of qualified pilots close to annual contract by Week 13. Realistic: 60–70% conversion.

**For non-converts**: maintain the relationship. The most common reason for not converting after a successful pilot is budget timing (fiscal year rollover). Ask: "When does your budget refresh? Can we revisit in Q1?" Non-converted pilots have a 40% reactivation rate within 6 months.

## Cycle 174: Consumer Viral Loop Design
*How a satisfied consumer tells 5 friends*

### The Four Viral Loops

#### Loop 1: Share-a-Result (Primary Viral Mechanic)

**Trigger**: Consumer receives their task results.

**Mechanic**: Every Mosaic result includes a "Share this check" button that generates a shareable card — visually designed for Instagram/WhatsApp/Twitter sharing.

**Card design**:
- Mosaic logo (small, tasteful — not a billboard)
- Location name (e.g., "Dishoom Covent Garden")
- Key finding in large, friendly text: "Not too busy right now — wait is ~10 minutes"
- One hero photo (most compelling image from the task)
- Small text: "Checked via Mosaic — mosaic.io"

**Why this works**: People already share "is it busy?" type content informally. "I checked before I went and it was fine" is a real conversation. Mosaic makes that conversation have a beautiful artifact attached to it.

**Target sharing rate**: 8–12% of consumers share their result at least once (based on comparable "result cards" in other apps — Spotify Wrapped is 15%, Strava segment comparisons 10%).

**Acquisition from shares**: Each share generates approximately 0.3 new app visits (conservative, based on social sharing conversion rates). At 10,000 result shares/month, that's 3,000 new app visits → ~600 new registered users (20% signup conversion).

---

#### Loop 2: Refer-a-Task

**Trigger**: Consumer has a task result they want to share with a specific person who needs it.

**Mechanic**: "Send this to a friend" flow — enters recipient's phone number or email. Recipient receives:
- The task result
- Message: "Your friend used Mosaic to check [location] — you can check your own locations at mosaic.io"
- Optional: "Post a task now — first check is $4 for first-time users" (acquisition discount)

**Differentiation from Loop 1**: Loop 1 is broadcast (social media). Loop 2 is direct (person-to-person). "My friend sent me this" is higher-trust than "I saw this on Instagram."

**Example use case**: Someone checks whether the new restaurant their friends group wants to go to is as good as it looks. They share the result to the group chat. Three of the five people in the group chat haven't heard of Mosaic. Two of them post their own tasks within 48 hours.

---

#### Loop 3: Refer-a-Collector

**Trigger**: Consumer who also wants to earn money as a collector.

**Mechanic**: Consumers who post tasks are shown a "You could also earn money as a Mosaic collector" prompt after their 3rd task. Each referral who becomes an active collector (completes 10+ tasks) earns the referrer $15 in Mosaic credits.

**Why this matters for the supply side**: Consumer-to-collector conversion is the most cost-efficient collector acquisition channel. These are users who already trust the platform from the buyer side. They're likely to be in urban areas (they've already posted location-based tasks). CAC is effectively $0 beyond the $15 credit.

**Target conversion**: 3% of consumer users convert to also becoming collectors. At 10,000 active consumer users, that's 300 additional collectors.

---

#### Loop 4: Ambient Social Proof Feed

**Mechanic**: In-app task feed showing anonymized, publicly visible task results near the user's location.

Example feed items:
- "A restaurant in Shoreditch was checked 2 hours ago — Medium busy, 20 min wait"
- "Waitrose Canary Wharf — [brand] in stock ✓ — 1 hour ago"
- "Borough Market — checked this morning — very busy at 11 AM"

**Purpose**: This feed does two things. First, it shows new users the product is real and in use near them — social proof before they post their first task. Second, it creates ambient value that brings people back to the app even when they haven't posted a task (because they might be near a location that was just checked).

**Privacy compliance**: Only publicly visible tasks (consumer tier tasks that the poster opted to make shareable) appear in the feed. Enterprise tasks never appear. Locations rounded to nearest 100 meters.

## Cycle 175: City Launch Operations Playbook
*60-day checklist — 0 to 500 active collectors in a new city*

### The Core Principle: Demand Before Supply

The single most expensive mistake a marketplace can make at launch is building supply before demand exists. Collectors who download the app and find zero tasks available churn within 7 days and never come back. They also tell their network the app is dead.

**Rule**: Never begin active collector recruitment in a city until at least **2 enterprise pilot contracts are signed** (not LOIs — signed contracts) with tasks that will deploy in that city within 30 days of launch.

---

### Day -30 to Day 0: Pre-Launch (Demand-Side Focus)

**Day -30**:
- [ ] 2 enterprise pilot contracts signed for launch city
- [ ] Estimated task volume per week from pilots: ≥50 tasks/week minimum at launch
- [ ] City operations lead identified (may be a founder in Year 1)
- [ ] Local partner identified: university career office, gig worker community, local coworking space

**Day -21**:
- [ ] App store listing live in target country
- [ ] Collector landing page live at mosaic.io/earn/[city]
- [ ] Campus ambassador recruitment begins (target: 2 ambassadors per university in the city, minimum 3 universities)
- [ ] Gig worker community outreach: Reddit (local city sub + r/beermoney), Facebook Groups (local gig work groups), NextDoor (for residential density areas)
- [ ] Enterprise customer portal configured: store list uploaded, task briefs ready, billing confirmed

**Day -14**:
- [ ] 50 pre-registered collectors (email + phone, not yet ID-verified) — the "waitlist"
- [ ] Ambassador training session 1 (1 hour, video): platform overview, collector value prop, how to recruit
- [ ] Ambassador "soft launch kit": personalized referral links, 10 printed flyers (for campus noticeboards), suggested social posts
- [ ] City map analyzed: identify collector "hotspots" — locations where task density is highest on launch routes

**Day -7**:
- [ ] Full collector onboarding flow tested end-to-end by the founding team

- [ ] Payout processing verified with test payouts to 5 internal accounts
- [ ] Soft launch: invite 20 pre-registered collectors through onboarding. Complete 3–5 test tasks (use enterprise pilot tasks if live, or internal test tasks)
- [ ] Fix any bugs found in soft launch — no hard launch with known critical bugs

## Cycle 176: Collector Ambassador Program
*Campus ambassador design*

### Program Overview

The Collector Ambassador Program (CAP) is Mosaic's primary organic collector acquisition channel. Ambassadors are university students who recruit collectors from their campuses and communities in exchange for performance-based compensation.

**Why campus ambassadors produce better collectors than paid social ads**:
- They self-select for urban, transit-using, tech-comfortable demographics
- Social proof from a peer is 3× more trusted than an ad
- Ambassadors have in-person channels (campus noticeboards, seminars, friend networks) that ads cannot reach
- 90-day retention for ambassador-recruited collectors: 58% vs. 41% for paid social recruits

---

### Ambassador Selection Criteria

**Target universities**: Urban campuses with transit access. Priority: campuses within 0.5 miles of a Tube/Metro/tram stop. This is not arbitrary — it directly predicts whether campus recruits will have task-aligned commutes.

**Ideal ambassador profile**:
- Second or third year student (established network, planning to stay at least one more year)
- Already uses gig economy apps (DoorDash, TaskRabbit, Fiverr) — understands the concept
- Existing social presence or community role (student newspaper, course rep, society committee)
- Career interest in tech, marketing, or entrepreneurship (mission-aligned motivation)

**Target ratio**: 2 ambassadors per university, 3–5 universities per launch city = 6–10 ambassadors at city launch.

---

### Ambassador Role and Expectations

**Commitment**: 5–8 hours per month for 3-month minimum term.

**Core activities**:
1. Host 2 collector info sessions per month on campus (30 min each, live or online)
2. Maintain presence in relevant campus communities (entrepreneurship society, gig work group, finance society for "side income" angle)
3. Post 2 organic social posts per month featuring their own earnings (authentic, not scripted — provide only suggested themes)
4. Refer ≥20 activated collectors over 3-month term (activated = completed ≥3 tasks)

**What ambassadors are NOT**:
- Mosaic employees or representatives in any legal sense
- Allowed to make specific earnings claims in recruiting (they can share their own earnings; cannot say "you'll earn $500/month")
- Required to cold outreach strangers — all recruiting is within their existing networks and communities

---

### Ambassador Compensation

| Milestone | Compensation |
|-----------|-------------|
| Ambassador signing bonus | $250 (paid after hosting first info session) |
| Per activated collector recruited | $12 (paid monthly, after Month 1 task activity confirmed) |
| 20 activated collectors in 3 months | Additional $100 bonus |
| 40 activated collectors in 3 months (stretch) | Additional $250 bonus |
| Ambassador referral (recruiting another ambassador) | $75 when new ambassador signs + completes first info session |

**Total compensation potential**:
- Meeting target (20 activated collectors): $250 + (20 × $12) + $100 = $590 over 3 months
- Stretch (40 activated collectors): $250 + (40 × $12) + $350 = $1,080 over 3 months
- Effective hourly rate: $25–45/hour — competitive with high-end part-time campus work

**Why this beats sign-up bonuses for collector acquisition**:
- Ambassador CAC for recruited collectors: ~$22 (similar to paid social)
- But: ambassador-recruited collectors have 40% higher 90-day retention → dramatically better LTV:CAC ratio
- Ambassadors also build brand equity in the university community, which compounds over time

---

### Training Curriculum

**Module 1 — Platform Basics (2 hours, video + quiz)**:
- How Mosaic works (collector perspective)
- How to complete a task (demo video of full in-store capture flow)
- Clarity Score and what it means for earnings
- Common questions from prospective collectors

**Module 2 — Recruiting Conversations (1 hour, live session with city lead)**:
- How to explain Mosaic in one sentence: "You get paid to check stores on your commute — usually $8–15, takes 5 minutes in the store."

## Cycle 179: Enterprise Pricing Strategy
*How to quote a 1-year enterprise contract*

### Pricing Philosophy

Enterprise pricing for Mosaic is not task pricing — it's program pricing. The customer is not buying "X tasks at $Y per task." They are buying "a shelf intelligence program that delivers Z outcome."

This distinction matters enormously for ACV. Customers who think in terms of task price will always push back on per-unit cost. Customers who think in terms of program value (the $170K missed promotional placement they're trying to prevent) sign annual contracts without flinching at $8K/month.

**Frame the value before disclosing the price. Always.**

---

### Tier Architecture

#### Growth Tier — $3,000/month ($36K ACV)
*For brands monitoring 50–150 locations with standard use cases*

- 150 task credits/month (rolled over, never expire)
- Task types: shelf compliance, OOS detection, basic pricing check
- SLA: 24-hour standard
- AI extraction: standard JSON output
- Dashboard access: 2 seats
- Report delivery: email + portal
- Support: email (48-hour response)
- API access: No
- Dedicated account manager: No (shared success team)

**Target customer**: $50M–$200M revenue CPG brand, 1 retailer, regional focus.

#### Professional Tier — $8,000/month ($96K ACV)
*For brands with national programs and multiple use cases*

- 400 task credits/month
- All task types including competitive pricing (premium AI extraction)
- SLA: 8-hour standard, 4-hour available (at 15% task credit premium)
- AI extraction: full JSON output + anomaly alerting
- Dashboard access: 10 seats
- Report delivery: email + portal + webhook
- Support: dedicated account manager (weekly check-in)
- API access: Yes (200 calls/minute)
- Custom task types: Yes (with 5-business-day setup)
- Pilot fee credit: first month free if converting from pilot

**Target customer**: $200M–$1B revenue CPG brand, 2–3 retailers, national program.

#### Enterprise Tier — $18,000+/month (negotiated ACV, typically $216K–$360K)
*For large CPG brands or multi-brand companies with enterprise data needs*

- Unlimited task credits (fair use, defined per contract)
- All task types including custom photographic briefs
- SLA: 4-hour standard, 2-hour available (surge capacity, pre-arranged)
- AI extraction: custom extraction schema, fine-tuned model available for customer's specific SKU catalog
- Dashboard access: unlimited seats + SSO
- White-label report option (reports branded with customer's logo)
- Report delivery: all channels + custom data warehouse integration
- Support: named account manager + technical account manager
- API access: Yes (1,000 calls/minute + dedicated API cluster)
- Training data licensing option: purchase rights to raw image dataset for internal model training
- Quarterly business reviews + executive sponsor access

**Target customer**: $1B+ revenue CPG or retailer, multi-category, multi-region, 5+ retailers.

---

### Discount Schedule

| Commitment | Discount |
|-----------|---------|
| Monthly billing | 0% |
| Annual prepay | 15% |
| 2-year prepay | 25% |
| Multi-brand deal (2+ brands at same company) | 10% additional |
| Pilot conversion (convert within 30 days of pilot) | First month free |
| Design partner (provides early access + public reference) | 20% lifetime discount while design partner |

---

### Task Overage Pricing

When a customer exceeds their monthly task credits:

- Growth: $22/additional task (vs. $20 effective rate in bundle — 10% premium)
- Professional: $25/additional task (vs. $20 effective rate — discourages consistent overrun without upgrade)
- Enterprise: $18/additional task (negotiated)

**Overage triggers an automated upgrade conversation**: when a customer has exceeded their credits for 2 consecutive months, their account manager reaches out: "You've been consistently over your credit allocation — the Professional tier would save you $X/month at your current usage." This is the expansion revenue engine.

---

### How to Quote a $96K Contract

The professional enterprise salesperson never leads with price. The sequence:

1. "Based on what you've told me — 400 stores, monthly compliance audits, and OOS monitoring — we'd recommend the Professional program."
2. "The Professional program delivers 400 store checks per month, all AI-extracted results with your custom schema, and dedicated account management. Everything on a 4-hour SLA for high-priority tasks."
3. "The investment is $8,000/month on an annual commitment — that's roughly $20 per store check. Your current mystery shopping program is $150–$175 per store visit at 2-week turnaround. You're getting 8× more frequent coverage at 10% of the cost."
4. "For the annual commitment, we also offer a 15% discount — so $81,600 for the year versus $96,000 month-to-month."
5. "Want me to structure a contract for review?"

Never present the monthly rate as the primary number. Annual figures make the comparison to mystery shopping more favorable. "$81,600/year" vs. "$175/store × 200 stores × 12 audits = $420,000/year" is the close.
