# Pixie — Naming Research

## Naming Criteria

1. Short (1–2 syllables preferred)
2. Memorable, easy to spell and say
3. Available domain (.ai or strong alternative)
4. No major trademark conflicts
5. Conveys intelligence, vision, ground truth, or field work
6. Works in B2B context (enterprise-appropriate)
7. Works in consumer/agent context (approachable)

---

## 10 Name Candidates

### 1. MOSAIC ⭐ RECOMMENDED

**Concept**: Many individual pieces forming a single coherent picture. Perfectly captures the crowdsourced-to-intelligence transformation.

**Strengths**:
- Self-explanatory metaphor: each agent captures a piece → Mosaic gives you the full picture
- Enterprise-appropriate (not cute or gimmicky)
- Works for both customer pitch ("see the full picture") and agent pitch ("you're part of the picture")
- Two syllables, easy to remember

**Domain**: mosaic.ai — likely available (not a dominant existing company). mosaicdata.ai or getmosaic.ai as fallbacks.

**Trademark risk**: LOW. "Mosaic" is a generic word used in many contexts. Mosaic Technologies, Mosaic Capital, Mosaic Software all exist but in different verticals. B2B data/intelligence space: no direct conflicts found.
> Note: Browser Mosaic (NCSA, 1993, defunct) — no trademark conflict. Mosaic Financial (investment firm) — different vertical.

**Tagline fit**: "The world, in real time." / "Ground truth at scale." / "The full picture."

---

### 2. CANVASS

**Concept**: "To canvass" = systematically gather information from a wide area. Alternate spelling from "Canvas" (the LMS software) avoids direct trademark conflict.

**Strengths**:
- Verb that describes exactly what agents do
- Professional, B2B-appropriate
- Alternate spelling differentiates

**Weaknesses**:
- Potential spelling confusion ("Is it canvas or canvass?")
- "Canvas" is a major software brand — some confusion risk even with different spelling

**Domain**: canvass.ai — likely available. canvassdata.ai as fallback.

**Trademark risk**: MEDIUM. Instructure's "Canvas" LMS is a major brand. "Canvass" (double-s) is a different word, but phonetically identical. In B2B enterprise sales context, confusion is a real risk.

---

### 3. GLIMPSE

**Concept**: On-demand glimpses into the physical world.

**Strengths**:
- Conveys the momentary, on-demand nature of capture
- Short, memorable, modern feel
- Consumer-friendly (appeals to agents)

**Weaknesses**:
- "Glimpse" implies incomplete view — not ideal for enterprise trust ("we just got a glimpse" sounds uncertain)
- Less professional than Mosaic

**Domain**: glimpse.ai — likely available. glimpsedata.ai as fallback.

**Trademark risk**: LOW. Glimpse.io is a small company, different space. Glimpse Group (AR/VR startup) — different vertical. Risk is minimal.

---

### 4. SCOUT

**Concept**: Scouts gathering intelligence, exploring terrain.

**Strengths**:
- Classic, strong metaphor
- Aligns with agent identity (agents are "scouts")
- Short (1 syllable)

**Weaknesses**:
- Scout24 (major German classifieds company) holds "Scout" trademark in EU — potential conflict in European expansion
- Many small companies use "Scout" — cluttered namespace

**Domain**: scout.ai — may be taken. scouted.ai, scoutdata.ai as fallbacks.

**Trademark risk**: HIGH for EU expansion due to Scout24. Acceptable for US launch.

---

### 5. FIELDWORK

**Concept**: Traditional academic/research fieldwork — systematic observation in the field.

**Strengths**:
- Highly descriptive and professional
- No cuteness — pure B2B credibility
- "Do fieldwork at scale" is a compelling enterprise pitch

**Weaknesses**:
- Three syllables — longer than ideal
- Descriptive names are hard to trademark

**Domain**: fieldwork.ai — likely available. thefieldwork.ai as fallback.

**Trademark risk**: LOW. Generic compound word. Some overlap with academic fieldwork software (Survey CTO, etc.) but different verticals.

---

### 6. VANTAGE

**Concept**: A vantage point — seeing from above, perspective-giving intelligence.

**Strengths**:
- Strong enterprise connotation
- "Vantage point" = strategic intelligence
- Professional, credible

**Weaknesses**:
- Vantage.ai, Vantage.com — both likely taken
- Common in financial/analytics space — generic feel

**Domain**: Likely taken in .ai and .com. Significant domain availability issue.

**Trademark risk**: MEDIUM-HIGH. Vantage is used broadly across many B2B software companies.

---

### 7. BEACON

**Concept**: A beacon guides and signals — Pixie agents are beacons of information.

**Strengths**:
- Strong metaphor for location + intelligence
- Enterprise-appropriate

**Weaknesses**:
- BeaconData, Beacon Capital, Beacon (health tech) — cluttered namespace
- Domain availability: beacondata.ai possible; beacon.ai likely taken

**Trademark risk**: MEDIUM. Too many companies named Beacon.

---

### 8. IRIS

**Concept**: The eye — visual intelligence.

**Strengths**:
- Perfect visual metaphor
- Short, elegant, memorable

**Weaknesses**:
- Iris.ai — taken (AI research tool)
- Many AI/tech companies use iris-related names
- May appear feminine, which is fine but limits perception in some enterprise contexts

**Domain**: iris.ai TAKEN. irisdata.ai possible but weak.

**Trademark risk**: HIGH. Iris.ai exists and operates in B2B space.

---

### 9. VERITAS

**Concept**: Latin for "truth" — ground truth intelligence.

**Strengths**:
- "Ground truth" = Veritas makes semantic sense
- Strong, credible, enterprise brand

**Weaknesses**:
- Veritas Technologies (formerly Veritas DGC) — major enterprise software company (backup/storage). Direct B2B conflict.
- Domain: veritas.com is taken by Veritas Technologies.

**Trademark risk**: VERY HIGH. Veritas Technologies is a large, well-known enterprise software company. Avoid.

---

### 10. LOCUS

**Concept**: A locus = a specific point in space; the point of origin. Locus Intelligence.

**Strengths**:
- Geographic/location connotation perfect for physical world intelligence
- Professional, clean

**Weaknesses**:
- Locus Robotics (warehouse automation robot company) has raised $400M+ and owns strong trademark
- Locus.sh (developer tool) also exists
- Potential confusion in B2B enterprise context

**Domain**: locus.ai — likely taken. Trademark conflict with Locus Robotics.

**Trademark risk**: HIGH due to Locus Robotics.

## Final Recommendation

### Primary: MOSAIC

**Full brand**: Mosaic
**Domain**: mosaic.ai (check availability; getmosaic.ai or mosaicdata.ai as fallbacks)
**Tagline**: "Ground truth at scale."
**Category claim**: Physical World Intelligence

**Why Mosaic wins**:
1. The metaphor is self-explaining ("every agent captures a piece → you get the full mosaic")
2. Enterprise-appropriate without being corporate-bland
3. Agent-side appeal ("you're building the mosaic")
4. Low trademark risk in B2B data/intelligence space
5. Works in all markets globally (recognized word in most languages)
6. Scales from a 10-customer company to a category-defining platform

**Customer pitch**: "Our agents capture pieces. Our AI assembles the mosaic. You see the full picture."

**Agent pitch**: "Every photo you take builds the Mosaic. Get paid for your piece."

---

## Brand Voice Guidelines

**Enterprise context**: "Ground truth at scale. Real-time physical world intelligence for the enterprise."

**Agent context**: "See your city. Get paid. Every photo you take tells a story."

**Competition framing**:
- vs Field Agent: "We give you insights, not just images."
- vs Trax: "No hardware. Any location. On demand."
- vs mystery shopping agencies: "Hours, not weeks. Machine-readable, not PDFs."

**Tone**: Confident, direct, no fluff. This is a data company, not a consumer app. The brand should feel like a trusted intelligence source, not a startup.
