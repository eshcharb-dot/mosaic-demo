Why Margins Improve

AI Cost Reduction

GPT-4o-mini at launch: $1.80/task. Fine-tuned Llama 70B at Year 3: $0.35/task. The training data that makes this possible is produced by every task Mosaic processes. This is a structural advantage that compounds — incumbents with legacy human review pipelines cannot replicate it.

Human Review Decline

15% of tasks require human QA at launch → 7% at 12 months → 2% at scale. Quality scoring AI improves on the same proprietary data. Each review produces a training label that makes the next model smarter.

Enterprise Mix Shift

Enterprise tasks at $25 command higher margins than SMB tasks at $12. As Mosaic adds enterprise customers, the blended task revenue increases from $18 (growth) to $20+ (scale) without needing to raise prices.

Pre-Empting the Hard Questions

The 20 Objections — Answered

Written after 50 cycles of research. Every sharp VC partner's likely objection, answered with data.

Q1"Why won't Google just do this with Street View and AI?"
Google Street View updates yearly, not hourly. CPG compliance monitoring requires same-day data — a Street View cadence is useless for detecting an OOS event that costs a brand $800K over 10 days. More importantly, Google does not sell $60K ACV B2B SaaS contracts to CPG field sales managers. It's a different motion entirely. Google's incentive is advertising — not field operations compliance data. The last time Google tried to enter enterprise field ops (Google Glass for manufacturing) it failed.
Q2"BeMyEye and Field Agent already do this. Why will you win?"
BeMyEye has 3M registered contributors, €25–35M ARR, and has not shipped a meaningful product update since 2020. No AI extraction. No API. No modern mobile UX. Their output is still raw photo galleries reviewed by humans — the same as it was in 2015. Field Agent is US-only, enterprise-only, with no consumer tier and no AI. We're not competing with them — we're replacing what they were supposed to become.
Q3"How do you acquire collectors at scale? DoorDash spent $1B doing this."
DoorDash paid drivers to go somewhere they wouldn't otherwise go, on demand, for $8. Our model is different: we assign tasks that are already on the collector's commute route. No detour, no car required. The acquisition channel is campus partnerships, commuter apps, and gig worker communities — not expensive ads. Year 1 collector acquisition budget: $500K for 3–5K monthly active collectors. Comparable: Field Agent reached similar scale with ~$1M in collector acquisition in Year 2.
Q4"What stops an enterprise customer from building this themselves?"
Two things: the collector network and the training data. To build the collector network independently, an enterprise customer needs to acquire, onboard, and manage 5,000+ gig workers across 6+ cities — a full marketplace company, not a software project. To match Mosaic's AI extraction accuracy, they'd need Mosaic's 2+ years of labeled image data. The build vs. buy math is clear: $1.5M/year for Mosaic vs. $15–20M in engineering + marketplace ops to replicate it.
Q5"Your collector numbers look too conservative. Why not 100K in Year 1?"
That's the right instinct reversed: we deliberately model conservatively to not get caught by pattern-matching. Every major gig platform (Uber, DoorDash, TaskRabbit, Mechanical Turk) took 10–15 years and billions in capital to reach 1–10M active workers. We're projecting 3–5K monthly active collectors at Year 1. Uber had comparable numbers at Year 2 with minimal capital. This is defensible, comparable-anchored, and passes VC scrutiny. 100K would end the meeting.
Q6"Is 44% gross margin good enough for a SaaS multiple?"
44% at launch is a marketplace gross margin, not a pure SaaS gross margin. The trajectory matters: 44% → 48% → 55%+ as AI costs fall and human review decreases. For comparison, DoorDash runs at 45–50% gross margin and traded at 6x revenue. Trax, the retail intelligence competitor, has reported ~50% gross margins at $100M ARR. The margin improvement story is central to our Series A raise narrative.
Q7"This sounds like a data business, not a software business. How do you get SaaS multiples?"
The enterprise portal and API are SaaS — subscription contracts, annual commitments, 120%+ NRR from cross-sell (shelf compliance → OOS detection → pricing intelligence is a natural upsell path). The data collection is the delivery mechanism, not the product. Comparably, Verint Systems is a data + software hybrid company that trades at 8x revenue. Near Intelligence (location data) traded at 5–7x at peak. We're building toward the higher-multiple tier by being the infrastructure layer, not just a data vendor.
