The Vision

We Are Blind

The founding insight behind Mosaic — and why it creates a generational company.

📷
mosaic-problem-blindness_v1.png

There are 6.8 billion smartphones on this planet. Each one has a camera, GPS, and more processing power than a 1990s supercomputer. Together they represent the most distributed sensor network ever assembled. And yet the physical world is almost entirely dark.

A brand manager in London is blind to what her products look like on a shelf in Lagos. A city planner in Berlin has no idea where people actually walk on Tuesday mornings. A blind person in São Paulo doesn't know whether the entrance ramp on her route is broken today.

The problem is not technological. GPS told us where things are. Mosaic tells us what things are. That shift — from coordinate to content — is the founding insight. And it's never been built at scale.

For the first time in human history, the physical world will have an answer.

The CPG Blind Spot

A typical Fortune 500 CPG brand has 50,000 retail doors in their distribution network. Their field team visits approximately 2,400 per year. For 95% of stores, they have no idea what their shelf looks like right now.

The Urban Planner's Gap

Cities spend $2B/year on urban mobility studies based on surveys and fixed counters. Real pedestrian patterns — which corners are crowded Tuesday at 8am — are simply unknown.

The Accessibility Void

1 billion people with mobility needs navigate a world where ramp status, queue length, and step count at their local destinations is not captured anywhere in any database.
