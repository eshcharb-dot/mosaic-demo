GDPR Deep Dive

The Regulatory Landscape — and Why On-Device Redaction Is the Key

✓ On-Device Redaction Solves

Image Personal Data

Post-redaction images contain no identifiable individuals. GDPR Recital 26 explicitly exempts anonymized data. If faces never leave the device unredacted, the uploaded image is not personal data. GDPR does not apply to it.

⚠ Still Required

Agent + Customer Data

Name, email, location, payment data — all require GDPR compliance: DPIA before each EU country launch, ROPA maintenance, DPAs with all sub-processors, EU Representative appointment (Art. 27), and Privacy Notices (Arts. 13/14).

Article 6 Breakdown

  • Customer data: Contract (6(1)(b))

  • Marketing to existing customers: Legitimate Interests

Mandate 2 · Cycles 126–128

The "Already Going There" Model

The highest cost in any gig economy is compensating workers for travel TO the location. Mosaic eliminates this. A commuter is already passing the Sainsbury's. A shopper is already at Whole Foods. The task meets the agent where they already are — and this changes everything about the economics.

🗺️

Mode 1: Trip Intent

I'm heading somewhere — show me what's on my route

Agent enters destination before leaving home. App computes route corridor and surfaces all tasks within 500m of the fastest path. Tasks ranked by payout × (1 - detour cost). Agent pre-accepts up to 5 tasks before commute begins. No reactive searching — proactive staging of entire earning session.

🚇

Mode 2: Commute Mode

Turn commute into earnings with one tap

Agent activates commute mode when leaving for work. System pre-loads tasks along the learned commute corridor overnight. "Accept All 3" button in a single tap. Each task triggers at geofence crossing. Agent earning £14.50 before arriving at office — zero dedicated travel time. This is the key retention mechanism.

🛒

Mode 3: Shopping Run

Tasks bundled at your destination store

Agent indicates they're heading to a specific store. App queues all tasks at that store AND nearby (500m). Multiple customers' tasks at same location — one agent completes 3 tasks in 3 minutes while grocery shopping. Highest earnings density mode on the platform.

📍

Mode 4: Already Here

Opportunistic capture when at a location

Standard proximity-based task feed for unplanned stops. Geofenced push notification: "You're near a task. 60 seconds, £4.00. Accept?" Modes 1–3 make this less necessary — commute-mode agents arrive at locations with tasks pre-accepted. This mode serves walk-ins and opportunistic agents.

The Micro-Task Innovation

If the agent is already at the location, there is no travel cost. The price floor is determined by task effort alone — not 15 minutes of transit time. This enables a new tier that doesn't exist in dispatched gig models: £2–3 tasks that are economic for both agent and platform because the agent's effective hourly rate is £72+ (6 tasks × £4 in 20 minutes vs £18/hr dispatched).

Technical Architecture
Route Matching Algorithm

Corridor-Based, Not Radius-Based

Compute route polyline → buffer 300–500m on each side → find tasks within corridor polygon → rank by Payout ÷ (1 + detour_coefficient). On-route tasks rank highest; detour tasks discounted proportionally. Zero server-side route storage — computed on device.

Privacy Architecture

Ephemeral Location Model

  • Route computation on-device — full polyline never sent to servers
  • GPS active only at trip start + task completion (< 10 sec/day total)
  • Route data cleared when commute mode disabled
  • Historical routes: opt-in, device-local only, deletable
  • No behavioral movement profiles on servers
Battery + Data Cost

Near-Zero Background Impact

GPS active: <10 seconds/day total. Background location: "significant location change" API only — wakes app when phone moves 500m+ (battery cost ≈ 0). Geofencing: native iOS/Android APIs, <0.1% additional battery per monitored region. Total data per commute session: <20KB. Uninstall risk from battery drain: minimal.

CEO Investor Relations Playbook

Fundraising is a relationship business. The CEOs who raise the best rounds don't start building VC relationships when they need money. They start 12 months before — and they treat every VC interaction as a long-term relationship, not a transaction.

01

Start 12 Months Early

A VC who has watched you execute for 12 months and hit every milestone trusts you. A VC who meets you for the first time the day you say "I'm raising" is betting on a stranger. The relationship converts at 40–60%. The cold pitch converts at 2–5%. The math is clear.

02

Give Before You Ask

Share market research. Make warm intros to deals they'd want. Write content they share. Show up to their events without pitching. Refer founders to their portfolio. Banked goodwill is the most valuable currency in fundraising — and it costs nothing to create.

03

The "Not Raising" First Meeting

"We're 8 months from our formal raise. We're asking for advice, not capital. What would you want to see us prove?" This removes pressure, gets honest feedback, and starts a relationship. Every specific piece of advice a VC gives becomes their investment in your success.

04

Be Honest in Updates

The most important section of a monthly update is "What didn't work." VCs who receive only good news don't trust the updates. VCs who receive honest failure accounts AND how you're addressing them update their model of you upward. Honesty compounds into trust.

05

Run a Process, Not a Pitch

Compress first meetings to a 2-week window. Set a close timeline. When the first term sheet arrives, immediately call the 3 other most interested investors personally. "We received a term sheet we're evaluating." FOMO without deception. The process creates the outcome.

+

Never Let a "No" End the Relationship

20–30% of investors who pass at pre-seed invest at seed or Series A when founders stayed in touch. After a pass: thank them specifically, ask to stay on the update list, execute on their feedback. The VC who passes today may lead your Series A in 18 months.
