VC Defense
Top VC Objections — With Responses
The 5 most critical objections and the data-backed responses developed in Cycle 104.
Field Agent has 200K agents and your advantages are incremental
Field Agent delivers photos. We deliver structured data. The difference is the JSON payload: SKU names, prices, shelf positions, compliance scores, C2PA proof. For enterprise buyers, this is "photos I have to review" vs "insights I act on." At 2-hour delivery vs 1–7 days, with cryptographic proof chain vs no verification, we're not incrementally better — we're a different product category. Field Agent is the Yellow Pages. We're building Google.
What happens when Uber or DoorDash adds photo tasks?
They can provide the network; they cannot provide the AI. Training shelf recognition models takes 18–24 months of labeled data. We'll have a 2-year head start. Enterprise customers with Mosaic integrated into Salesforce and 24 months of historical baseline data are not switching because Uber has drivers. And gig worker psychology mismatch: DoorDash drivers optimize for speed; our agents are detail-oriented. Different supply pool. If Uber enters, we'd likely be acquired — also a win.
60% take rate will compress when competition enters
Our take rate is an AI processing fee, not a pass-through margin. Uber takes 28% on commodity delivery. We take 60% because we're delivering structured AI-extracted intelligence, not raw photos. A competitor entering at 40% delivers photos. We deliver decisions. NielsenIQ charges $500K–$2M/year for retail intelligence that's 6 weeks old. Our margin could compress 20 points and we'd still be the best value proposition in the market.
Enterprise concentration risk — 10 customers is not a business
The three-tier model eliminates this risk by design. By Month 18: 10,000 consumer users ($220K/month T1) + 600 SMB accounts ($242K/month T2) + 50 enterprise accounts ($275K/month T3). Enterprise drops to 33% of revenue. One enterprise customer churning = 0.6% revenue hit, not 10%. Consumer and SMB are the churn buffer that makes enterprise losses survivable.
Training data market is commoditizing via Scale AI and Appen
We're selling a fundamentally different product. Scale AI sells labeled images from stock photos and generic datasets. We sell verified real-world imagery with GPS+C2PA provenance, domain-specific labels (retail shelf, property damage, urban environments), and ground-truth accuracy from our AI pipeline. Waymo paid $100M+ for proprietary simulation data. Databricks acquired MosaicML at $1.3B partly for training data. The premium market for authentic, verified, domain-specific data is growing — we're not competing with Appen for generic labeling jobs.

Strategic Innovation
8 Interesting Combinations
Non-obvious combinations of customer × use case × business model. These are the venture design ideas that don't exist anywhere today.
01

Multi-Buyer Single Capture

Compound take rate model

One agent visit fulfills tasks for 3 different buyers simultaneously — without knowing about each other. Instead of platform taking 60% of one $15 task ($9), platform takes 60% of three $10 micro-tasks ($18). Same physical work, 2× revenue. Same visit serves a CPG brand check, an insurance exterior doc, and a real estate foot traffic study.

02

Consumer-Sponsored Enterprise Task

Dual-sided marketplace — brand subsidizes consumer tasks

Jake Torres (hot sauce brand) sets up a co-sponsorship: any consumer photographing a Texas grocery shelf gets $3 off their task cost — because Fuego Foods gets a copy of the resulting photo. Consumer pays less. Agent earns same. Mosaic earns from both sides. This is programmatic advertising applied to physical world data collection. Never existed before.

03

The Agent Who Doesn't Know They're Valuable

Ambient intelligence premium — secondary data monetization

A routine restaurant scout captures background data: a billboard that hasn't been posted yet, a new menu item, unusual foot traffic. AI surfaces this incidental value to secondary buyers. OOH company needs billboard monitoring. CPG brand that supplies the restaurant wants menu data. Agent earns a bonus for incidental captures. Platform earns a coordination fee from secondary buyers.

04

The Customer Who Is Also an Agent

Intelligence loop — same location monetized twice

A local pharmacy owner uses Mosaic to monitor CVS and Walgreens. CVS corporate uses Mosaic to monitor independent pharmacies in their expansion markets. Neither knows the other is a customer. Both pay Mosaic to spy on each other. The platform monetizes the same physical location twice — from both sides of a competitive relationship.

05

The Bounty Pool

Crowdfunded intelligence — Kickstarter for location data

A single location (e.g., Apple Store Fifth Avenue) is too expensive for one buyer at $500/day. Four buyers — real estate firm, OOH advertiser, investment researcher, journalist — each contribute $100–150/day to a shared bounty. Agent earns from the pool. Each buyer gets their specific data extraction. Platform takes a 30% coordination fee. One expensive task becomes four affordable ones.

06

Subscribe to a Location, Not a Task Type

Geo-fence subscription — location as the product

Instead of "I need 500 shelf audits," the customer says "I want to subscribe to Times Square." A geo-fenced area gets continuous agent deployment — crowd levels, billboard presence, retail promotions, construction changes, foot traffic patterns — all captured by any agent who passes through. $500–5,000/month per location. Real estate investors, hedge funds, and city planners pay for continuous location intelligence.

07

Social Intelligence / Ambient Earn

Waze for physical world — earn by documenting where you already go

Emma is in Barcelona visiting the Sagrada Família. Mosaic surfaces: "Earn $8: Take 3 photos of the current entrance queue." She was going there anyway. The buyer: a tourism analytics company wanting real-time queue data. The app recommends tasks that match your location and current intent. Every tourist, every commuter, every local becomes a passive Mosaic agent for places they already visit.

08

Intelligence Feed for Hedge Funds

Alternative data subscription — the Amazon selling compute moment

500,000 tasks/month across 100 cities creates aggregate signals: retail foot traffic trends, CPG promotion intensity by region, construction activity by city, real estate site activity. Anonymized and indexed by location + time. Sold as an alternative data subscription to hedge funds and investment research firms at $50K–$500K/year. Pure data product revenue from the existing task flow. This is the Series B story.
