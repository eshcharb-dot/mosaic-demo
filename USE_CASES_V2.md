## Cycle 106: Use Case Brainstorm — 30 New Ideas

### Category A: Individual Consumer Use Cases

1. **"Scout this restaurant for me"** — Someone going on a first date or business dinner wants current photos of the ambiance, noise level, wait time, and menu board. Posts a $10 task. Gets back photos + AI assessment within 2 hours.

2. **"Is the park crowded?"** — Parent wants to know if the local park/beach/trail is busy before packing up the kids. Posts a $3–5 task for a quick video pan of the park. Real-time crowding without infrastructure investment.

3. **"Check my Airbnb neighborhood"** — Traveler arriving in a new city wants to verify the neighborhood looks safe, the street is as described, and the building is real. Posts a task before booking or before arrival.

4. **"Is my contractor actually working?"** — Homeowner with a contractor on site and no way to check in. Posts a task for an exterior photo of the property to verify work is in progress.

5. **"Parking availability at the venue"** — Concert/game attendee wants to know parking lot occupancy before driving. Posts a $5 task for a 60-second parking lot scan.

6. **"Line length at [restaurant/store/attraction]"** — Anyone who hates waiting wants to know the current line before committing to drive there. Spontaneous, high-frequency consumer need.

7. **"Verify the product is actually on the shelf before I drive there"** — Consumer wants to know if a specific item is in stock at a specific store before making the trip. E-commerce has this data; brick-and-mortar doesn't.

8. **"Scout location for my photoshoot/video"** — Photographer, filmmaker, or content creator wants current photos of a potential shoot location they haven't visited yet.

### Category B: Small Business and Indie Brand Use Cases

9. **"How does my shelf display look?"** — A small brand with 50 SKUs in 200 stores can't afford enterprise mystery shopping. Posts a $10 task to see their display at any specific store, on demand.

10. **"How's my competitor's display?"** — Same brand, targeting competitor's shelf space. "What does Cholula's hot sauce section look like at the Whole Foods on West 14th St?"

11. **"Is my outdoor signage visible from the street?"** — New local business wants to verify their new sign is visible from the sidewalk and street before spending more on advertising.

12. **"Check my competitor's new location before we respond"** — A regional restaurant chain opens a competitor across the street. Owner wants daily exterior photos to track their launch traffic.

13. **"Document this property before I buy it"** — Real estate investor or agent wants a thorough photo documentation of a property they can't visit personally. $30–50 for a complete exterior walkthrough.

14. **"Verify my franchise location's standards"** — Franchisor wants to verify that a franchise location is meeting brand standards (signage, cleanliness, displays) without sending an official inspector.

### Category C: Events and Entertainment

15. **"How crowded is the venue entrance before I arrive?"** — Live event attendee wants real-time photos of the venue entry queue 30 minutes before they leave home.

16. **"Live street view during the festival"** — Festival-goers and brands sponsoring events want real-time crowd coverage from multiple angles during the event.

17. **"Document the setup/teardown of my event"** — Event planners want photographic documentation of venue setup at T-minus-2 hours that they can't be physically present for.

18. **"Coverage from angles I can't cover"** — Sports event organizers want supplemental photography from fan perspective angles that official photographers don't have access to.

### Category D: Travel and Tourism

19. **"What does the hotel lobby look like right now?"** — Traveler checking in wants to see the current state of the hotel, not the marketing photos from 5 years ago.

20. **"Is the attraction worth it today?"** — Tourist wants to know if the Louvre is packed today, if the weather has affected visibility at the Grand Canyon, if the famous restaurant has a line.

21. **"Scout my road trip stops"** — Someone planning a road trip wants current photos of rest stops, scenic overlooks, and local attractions before committing to a route.

### Category E: Journalism and Research

22. **"Eyes on this location"** — Journalist covering a breaking story wants boots-on-ground photos from a location without dispatching a staff photographer. Citizen journalist model, monetized.

23. **"Track this construction project"** — Local journalist or civic watchdog wants weekly progress photos of a controversial development or infrastructure project.

24. **"Document this protest/event as it happens"** — Media organization wants multiple-angle citizen documentation of a public event in real time.

25. **"Academic field research at scale"** — Researcher studying urban environments, retail behavior, or public space usage needs photos from 100 locations across 10 cities for their study.

### Category F: Environmental and Safety

26. **"Flood/storm damage assessment"** — Insurance company or government agency deploys Mosaic agents to document storm damage across a region within hours of a weather event.

27. **"Verify no illegal dumping at this site"** — Municipal government or environmental org needs regular photo documentation of a known dumping site.

28. **"Check this trail/road condition"** — Outdoor recreation platform (AllTrails, Waze) wants real-time condition reports on trails and roads beyond what user-submitted data provides.

29. **"Document this safety hazard"** — An anonymous reporter wants to document a hazardous condition (broken sidewalk, illegal signage, health code violation) with verified photo proof for a complaint.

### Category G: Health and Wellness + Other

30. **"Is the gym packed right now?"** — Gym-goer wants to know if their local gym is at capacity before making the trip. Gym chains don't publish this data. ($3 consumer task — extremely high frequency)

### Use Case 9: Restaurant Scout ("Is This Place Worth It?")

**Customer Persona**
**Name**: Alex Morgan, 31
**Situation**: Planning a first date at a restaurant he's never been to. Found it on Yelp with 4.3 stars but the photos are 3 years old. Wants to know: Is it crowded on a Saturday night? What's the vibe like? Is the lighting romantic? Is there a parking situation?

**The Task**
"Visit [Restaurant Name] at [address] on Saturday night between 7–8pm. Capture: 3–5 photos of the dining room, 1 video pan showing noise level / ambiance, 1 photo of the entrance/wait area showing current line length, 1 outdoor photo showing parking situation."

**What AI Extracts**
- Crowd density score: low/medium/high (based on visible seat occupancy)
- Ambiance classification: romantic/casual/lively/loud (from lighting + table spacing + decor analysis)
- Wait estimate: visible queue length + estimated wait time
- Noise proxy: video analysis detecting conversation volume ranges (AI can estimate this from mouth movement patterns)
- Parking density: available spots visible in frame

**What They Get Back**
A "Pre-Visit Intelligence Card":
- 5 verified real-time photos
- "Vibe Score": romantic/casual/lively on a 1–5 scale
- Current wait: ~15 minutes
- Crowd level: 70% full
- Parking: street parking available
- "Agent Note": "Place feels upscale but relaxed. Not too noisy. Good for conversation."

**How They Find Mosaic (Self-Serve)**
- Google search: "restaurant scout app" or "check restaurant before going"
- TikTok: influencer posts "I used Mosaic to scout a restaurant before my date and here's what I found"
- App Store discovery: "Neighborhood Intelligence" category
- Partnership with OpenTable, Yelp as a "real-time intel" add-on

**What They Pay**
$8–$15 per restaurant scout. One-time credit card charge. No subscription needed.

**ROI / Value to Customer**
- Avoids a $200 dinner at a loud, crowded, or disappointing restaurant
- Creates confidence and impresses the date ("I actually checked it out in advance")
- Emotional value: reduced anxiety about unknown situations

**Regulatory Notes**
Restaurant interior photography by agents who are present as customers: legally equivalent to any diner taking photos of their food (ubiquitous practice). No privacy issues if faces are redacted. Noise complaints: the agent is a paying customer at a restaurant — zero legal exposure.

**Virality Mechanism**
Agent shares the "Pre-Visit Card" on Instagram/TikTok: "I got paid $12 to scout a restaurant for someone's date night." Consumer shares: "Used Mosaic before my date — it's like having a spy." Both sides create organic content.

### Use Case 10: Consumer In-Stock Check ("Is It Actually There?")

**Customer Persona**
**Name**: Keisha Williams, 38
**Situation**: Looking for a specific baby formula brand that's been out of stock everywhere in her city. Needs to know which store has it before driving across town. Has spent 3 hours checking websites (all say "check availability in store") and calling stores (all say "yes, we have it" but never do).

**The Task**
"Go to the baby formula/infant food aisle at [Store Name, Address]. Check if [Specific Brand + SKU] is in stock. Photograph the full shelf section. If it's in stock, note how many units are available."

**What AI Extracts**
- Specific product present: Yes/No
- Quantity visible: exact unit count estimate
- Alternative options: similar products visible if target is out of stock
- Price: current price tag
- Location: exact shelf position for when she arrives

**What They Get Back**
A simple "Stock Alert":
- "Enfamil Gentlease 30oz: IN STOCK — approximately 7 units available"
- Current photo of the shelf section
- Price: $34.99
- "Also available nearby: Similac Sensitive (12 units)"
- Captured at: 2:47 PM, 15 minutes ago

**How They Find Mosaic**
- Facebook mom groups (high-trust referral environment)
- Reddit r/BabyBumps, r/beyondthebump (viral post: "I used this app to find baby formula")
- Parenting blogs
- Brick-and-mortar stores' own apps as an integration

**What They Pay**
$5–8 per stock check. Or a "Stock Alert" subscription: $19/month for unlimited stock checks in one metro area (power use case: parents, collectors, sneakerheads, gamers looking for launch-day stock).

**ROI / Value**
- Saves 2–3 hours of store-calling and driving
- Emotional relief is enormous for a parent who needs formula
- High repeat purchase: same family will use this weekly for various stock issues

**Regulatory Notes**
Same as retail shelf audit — photographing publicly displayed products with price tags. Zero privacy issues.

**Virality**
The "found it!" moment is highly shareable in parenting communities. "This app found me baby formula when no one else could" is an extremely shareable story. One viral Reddit post could drive thousands of downloads.

### Use Case 11: Small Brand Shelf Intelligence (Self-Serve)

**Customer Persona**
**Name**: Jake Torres, 42
**Title**: Founder/Owner, Fuego Foods (regional hot sauce brand)
**Situation**: 47 SKUs in 200 stores across Texas and Louisiana. Just expanded into Whole Foods Market. His distributor told him the placement is "great." He drove to the Houston flagship and found his product on the bottom shelf, facing backward, with a competitor's display facing forward at eye level. He needs to know what's happening in the other 199 stores.

**The Task**
"Go to [Store Name, Address]. Find the hot sauce / condiment aisle. Photograph the full section, then zoom in on Fuego Foods products if present. Capture: shelf position, number of facings, any out-of-stock gaps, competitor displays adjacent to ours."

**What AI Extracts**
- Fuego Foods products: present/absent
- Shelf position: eye level/mid level/bottom level
- Number of facings: exact count
- Share of shelf vs competitors: percentage
- Promotional compliance: is the price tag correct?
- Any OOS: flagged immediately

**What They Get Back**
A "Brand Pulse Report" — a simple PDF or dashboard showing all completed audits for the month, with a store-by-store status grid:
- Green = correct placement, in stock
- Yellow = present but compliance issues (wrong position, missing facings)
- Red = out of stock or not found

**How Jake Finds Mosaic and Buys Without a Sales Call**
1. Sees a Mosaic ad in a Shopify merchant community or Faire wholesale platform
2. Visits mosaic.com/brands
3. Sees pricing: $10/task, minimum $50 (5 tasks)
4. Enters credit card, enters store addresses, posts tasks
5. Gets results within 24–48 hours
6. Never speaks to a salesperson

**What He Pays**
- Per task: $10–15
- "Brand Monitor" plan: $99/month for 10 recurring monthly audits (same stores, automated)
- "Growth" plan: $299/month for 30 audits + analytics dashboard

**ROI**
- Jake's distributor fee is 18% of revenue. Without shelf intelligence, he has no leverage in distribution conversations.
- One OOS finding caught early = protecting sales he'd otherwise lose
- "I charge distributors a compliance fee if they fail to maintain our placement — I needed data to enforce this. Mosaic gave it to me."

**Regulatory Notes**
Same as enterprise shelf audit. Legal, standard practice.

**Customer Persona**
**Name**: Various — this is the highest-frequency consumer use case
Personas:
- **Parent**, 35, wants to know if the local playground/park is busy before driving with 2 kids
- **Gym member**, 27, wants to know if the gym is at capacity before leaving work
- **Student**, 22, wants to know if the popular bar is too crowded at 10pm
- **Shopper**, 45, wants to know if the Saturday market is worth the drive

**The Task**
"Stand at the entrance to [Location, Address] and take a 30-second video pan showing the current crowd level. Also one photo showing the parking situation."

**What AI Extracts**
- People count: estimated headcount in frame (faces automatically redacted)
- Density classification: empty/light/moderate/busy/packed
- Wait estimate (for queues): visible queue length
- Parking: available/limited/full

**What They Get Back**
A "Crowd Card" — optimized for mobile consumption:
- Current density: BUSY (estimated 60–80 people)
- Wait time: ~10 minutes (based on visible queue)
- Parking: Street parking limited, lot has spaces
- Best time to visit: based on historical pattern data (coming feature)
- Captured: 8 minutes ago

**How They Find Mosaic**
This is the high-frequency feature that justifies a consumer app download:
- "Check before you go" as a category in the app store
- Google search: "is [place name] busy right now"
- Integration with Google Maps, Apple Maps as an "ask Mosaic" button
- Partnership with Yelp, Foursquare

**What They Pay**
- Per check: $3–5
- Monthly pack: $9.99/month for 10 checks (gym membership crowd checker tier)

**The Frequency Play**
This is the gateway drug for Mosaic consumer adoption. A user who uses Mosaic to check their gym 4 times a week will also:
- Scout a restaurant before a date
- Check if the park is crowded
- Ask someone to verify their parking situation before driving

**Virality**
The crowd level is inherently social: "Don't go to Trader Joe's right now, it's insane" is a text people send each other. The Mosaic crowd card becomes that text. Shareable, specific, current.

## Cycle 109: The "Interesting Combinations" Analysis

*8 non-obvious combinations of customer × use case × business model*

---

### Combination 1: The Multi-Buyer Single Capture
**Concept**: One physical location, one captured moment, three different buyers pay for it simultaneously — without knowing about each other.

**How It Works**:
A Mosaic agent captures a shelf in a Walmart in Houston. In the task queue at that moment:
- CPG Brand A has requested competitor price monitoring for that exact Walmart
- Insurance Company B has requested building exterior documentation at a nearby address and includes the Walmart exterior
- Real Estate Agency C has requested foot traffic documentation for potential nearby site

The agent captures photos that partially fulfill all three requests. The AI engine matches the photos to all relevant pending tasks and delivers partial fulfillment. Each buyer receives what they requested. The agent earns 3× the base rate for one visit.

**Business Model Angle**: Platform takes a cut of each transaction. The same visit generates $30+ revenue instead of $10. Margin improves because agent time is the same.

**This is a compound take rate model**: Instead of platform taking 60% of one $15 task ($9), platform takes 60% of three $10 micro-tasks ($18). Same physical work, 2× revenue.

**Unlock Required**: Task overlap detection algorithm. Agent app UI that shows "this visit covers 3 tasks."

---

### Combination 2: The Consumer-Sponsored Enterprise Task
**Concept**: An individual posts a task. A brand "co-sponsors" the task to get a copy of the data.

**How It Works**:
Jake Torres (hot sauce brand) sets up a "data co-sponsorship" in Mosaic. Any consumer who posts a task that photographs a grocery shelf in Texas gets a $3 discount — because Jake's company gets a copy of the resulting shelf photo.

Consumer posts: "Check if Trader Joe's has the new hot sauce brand on the shelf." Cost: normally $8, but discounted to $5 because Fuego Foods is sponsoring (and gets the photo).

Fuego Foods pays $3 per co-sponsored task to get shelf intelligence gathered by consumers who were already going to buy it. Consumer pays less. Agent earns same. Mosaic earns from both sides.

**Business Model**: Consumer-sponsored marketplace. Brand pays CPM (cost per relevant capture). This is programmatic advertising applied to physical world data collection.

**This has never existed before**: A model where a brand effectively subsidizes consumer curiosity in exchange for physical world data points. Like Google AdWords but for shelf photos.

---

### Combination 3: The Agent Who Doesn't Know They're Valuable
**Concept**: Agent captures data they didn't know was commercially valuable until AI surfaces it.

**How It Works**:
A Mosaic agent does a routine restaurant scout task. The AI, analyzing the background of the photos, detects:
- A competitor's outdoor advertising hasn't been posted yet (it's still rolled up)
- There's a new product on the restaurant's menu board that was just added
- Foot traffic near the restaurant has unusually changed pattern

This incidental data is valuable to different buyers:
- OOH advertising company: did their placement go up?
- CPG brand that supplies the restaurant: what's on the menu?
- Real estate investment trust: how is foot traffic changing in this corridor?

**Business Model**: "Ambient intelligence premium." The agent earns a bonus when their incidental captures generate secondary value. Platform earns a bonus revenue share when secondary buyers purchase the data.

**Unlock Required**: An AI layer that analyzes all submitted photos for secondary value beyond the primary task, and a "data marketplace" where secondary buyers can bid for specific types of incidental captures.

---

### Combination 4: The Customer Who Is Also an Agent
**Concept**: A store owner monitors competitors using Mosaic as a customer. A competitor's store owner does the same. Both are customers AND contributing agents (unknowingly) to each other's intelligence.

**How It Works**:
A local pharmacy owner in Nashville uses Mosaic to monitor competitor CVS and Walgreens in their neighborhood. Posts tasks to check their prices, promotions, and product availability.

Meanwhile, CVS corporate uses Mosaic to monitor independent pharmacies in their expansion markets. CVS's agents are capturing data from the independent pharmacy as part of their competitor monitoring tasks.

Neither customer knows the other is a customer. Both contribute to a richer data ecosystem.

**Business Model Angle**: "Intelligence loop" — every customer pays for intelligence about their competitors. Every customer's environment generates intelligence that competitors are paying for. The platform monetizes the same physical location twice.

**Privacy Note**: All data is sold only to the requesting customer. No cross-contamination of competitor intelligence. But the platform builds a rich physical world dataset from the overlap.

---

### Combination 5: The Bounty Pool — Multiple Customers Co-Fund One Expensive Task
**Concept**: When a single task is too expensive for one buyer, multiple buyers contribute to a "bounty pool" that funds it.

**How It Works**:
A Mosaic user wants continuous monitoring of the Apple Store on Fifth Avenue in New York — foot traffic, product display changes, promotional activity, line lengths. This would cost $500/day for 12-hour coverage.

The platform creates a "bounty" for this location. Multiple interested parties contribute:
- A retail real estate firm: contributes $100/day for foot traffic data
- An OOH advertiser: contributes $100/day for promotional presence monitoring
- An investment research firm: contributes $150/day for consumer behavior intelligence
- A journalist: contributes $50/day for "is the new iPhone launch packed?"

Total bounty: $400/day. Agent earns $160/day. Platform earns $240/day margin. Each buyer gets their specific data extraction. Everyone wins.

**Business Model**: Crowdfunded intelligence. Like Kickstarter for location data. The platform takes a 30% coordination fee on top of the task cost.

**Unlock Required**: A "location bounty" marketplace where buyers can see pending tasks they can co-fund for a lower per-buyer price, or launch their own bounty.

---

### Combination 6: Subscribe to a Location, Not a Task Type
**Concept**: Instead of buying "retail shelf audit tasks," a customer subscribes to a specific location.

**How It Works**:
Instead of: "We need 500 shelf audits/month at random stores"
The customer says: "We want to subscribe to Times Square, NYC — give us everything that happens there."

A "Location Subscription" is a geo-fenced area where Mosaic continuously deploys agents to capture all relevant data:
- Crowd level changes throughout the day
- Billboard/OOH advertising presence
- Restaurant/retail promotions visible from street
- Construction/infrastructure changes
- Weather-related conditions
- Events or unusual activity

Buyers: real estate investors, city planners, hedge funds, retail brands with flagship locations.

**Business Model**: $500–5,000/month per location subscription. The platform routes any agent who passes through that geo-fence to capture standard "location intelligence" photos. Revenue generated regardless of which agent happens to be there.

**The key insight**: The asset is the location, not the task. Many buyers want continuous intelligence about specific locations. No one offers this as a subscription product today.

---

### Combination 7: The Social Intelligence Play — Earning by Documenting Your Own Life
**Concept**: Users opt in to earn credits for documenting places they're already going, and that documentation gets purchased by brands.

**How It Works**:
Emma (the Airbnb traveler) is in Barcelona. She visits the Sagrada Família. She opens Mosaic and sees: "Earn $8: Take 3 photos of the current visitor entrance queue and crowd level."

She was going there anyway. She takes the photos. Earns $8. The buyer? A tourism analytics company or a travel app that wants real-time queue data.

This turns every tourist, every commuter, every local into a potential Mosaic agent for tasks that match where they're already going.

**Business Model**: "Ambient earn." The platform recommends tasks that match your location and intent, rather than requiring you to seek out tasks. This is Waze (crowdsourced, passive) applied to visual intelligence.

**Virality**: "I earned $47 on my Barcelona vacation just by taking photos of places I was already visiting" is viral travel content.

---

### Combination 8: The Data Flywheel as a Product — "Intelligence Feed" for Hedge Funds / Investment Research
**Concept**: The aggregate of all Mosaic tasks across thousands of locations creates a "physical world intelligence feed" that investment research firms will pay for.

**How It Works**:
Mosaic processes 500,000 tasks/month across 100 cities. The aggregate output creates signals that individual customers never asked for:
- Retail foot traffic trends across neighborhoods
- Out-of-stock rates by product category and region
- CPG brand promotional intensity (how many stores have a promotion running this week)
- Construction activity by city and district
- Real estate site activity (how many people are looking at properties in an area)

This aggregate feed is anonymized, indexed by location and time, and sold as a subscription data product to:
- Hedge funds doing alternative data investing (they pay $50K–$500K/year for proprietary datasets)
- Consumer research firms (Forrester, Gartner equivalents for physical retail)
- Government policy researchers
- Urban planning departments

**Business Model**: "Alternative data feed." Priced at $10K–$200K/year depending on coverage and refresh frequency. This is a B2B data product built on top of the task marketplace.

**This is the "Amazon selling compute" moment**: The platform built to serve customers (CPG brands, insurers) produces a meta-product (intelligence feed) that's more valuable than the primary product. This is the Series B story.

### Minimum Task Price to Make It Work

- Agent earns 40% → minimum $4 agent payout needed to attract quality agents for a 10-minute task

### Credit/Wallet Model vs Subscription for Irregular Users

**Credit Wallet** (for irregular users):

- Best for: brand owners monitoring specific stores monthly

**Enterprise** (for power buyers):

### The Freemium Tier

**Free tier exists**: First 3 tasks free (for new users only).

**Rationale for why we reversed the "no free tier" decision**:
The enterprise business should not have a free tier (this devalues enterprise pricing). The consumer/SMB business MUST have one (self-serve products that require credit cards from strangers need a trust-building moment).

**Free tier design**:
- 3 free tasks, limited to crowd level / exterior photo types
- Results delayed by 24 hours (creates upgrade urgency)
- Full results (including AI extraction) require paid upgrade
- Visible at bottom of results: "Upgrade for instant results + full AI analysis"

**Conversion target**: 25% of free-tier users to paid within 14 days.
