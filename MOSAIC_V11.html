The Founding Thesis

We Are Building the OS Layer for Physical Reality

Not a CPG analytics tool. Not a gig economy app. The infrastructure platform that makes the physical world machine-readable — at scale, on demand, permanently.

There are 6.8 billion smartphones on this planet. Each one has a camera, GPS, and more compute than a 1990 supercomputer. Together they are the most distributed sensor network ever assembled. And the physical world is still almost entirely dark.

A brand manager in London is blind to what her products look like on a shelf in Leeds. A CPG director in New York doesn't know that 600 of his stores have been out of stock for eight days. An insurance adjuster can't see the flood damage on the property she's underwriting. A city planner can't verify the accessible ramp he approved was built to spec. Every sector that touches the physical world is making decisions in the dark.

The problem is not technological. GPS told us where things are. Mosaic tells us what things are. That shift — from coordinate to content — is the founding insight. And it has never been built at scale. $35B is spent annually on physical world intelligence. Most of it produces data that's already outdated when it arrives.

We are not building another field intelligence product. We are building the infrastructure layer that sits between the physical world and every organization that needs to understand it. The sensors exist. The AI to interpret them exists. The platform to organize human attention into structured, machine-readable truth at a price and speed that makes physical world intelligence routine — that has never existed. Until now.

Mosaic is that platform. CPG is the wedge. Physical reality is the market.
