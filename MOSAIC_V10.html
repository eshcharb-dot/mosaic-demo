The Founding Thesis

We're Building Physical World Intelligence Infrastructure

Not a CPG analytics tool. Not a gig economy app. The infrastructure layer that makes the physical world machine-readable — permanently.

There are 6.8 billion smartphones on this planet. Each one has a camera, GPS, and more processing power than a 1990s supercomputer. Together they represent the most distributed sensor network ever assembled. And yet the physical world is almost entirely dark.

A brand manager in London is blind to what her products look like on a shelf in Leeds. A CPG director in New York doesn't know that 600 of his stores have been out of stock for eight days. An insurance adjuster can't see the flood damage on the property she's underwriting. A city planner can't verify the accessible ramp he approved was built to spec.

The problem is not technological. GPS told us where things are. Mosaic tells us what things are. That shift — from coordinate to content — is the founding insight. And it's never been built at scale.

We are not building a field intelligence product. We are building the infrastructure platform that sits between the physical world and every organization that needs to understand it. The data exists. The sensors exist. The AI to interpret them exists. What has never existed is the platform that organizes human attention into structured, machine-readable truth at a price and speed that makes physical world intelligence routine, not heroic.

Mosaic is that platform. We make the physical world legible for the first time. Starting in CPG. Ending everywhere.
