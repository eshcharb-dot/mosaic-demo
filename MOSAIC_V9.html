Competitive Intelligence

Competitor Autopsies

Primary research across 10 cycles. Every major incumbent analyzed for product, pricing, customers, and — most importantly — why they can't respond.

BeMyEye
Founded 2012 · Milan · €30M raised · ~3M registered contributors · Estimated €20–25M ARR (declining)
Declining Incumbent

What They Built

The European pioneer in crowdsourced retail intelligence. Correctly identified the CPG field coverage gap in 2012 and built a platform that attracted Nestlé, PepsiCo, Henkel as customers. Raised €30M, grew to 3M contributors, reached €35M ARR by 2021. Then stopped innovating.

Their contributor app: functional but dated. Last major redesign: 2020 based on App Store screenshots. No real-time AI validation on-device.

Why They Can't Catch Up

No AI extraction — output is still raw photo galleries reviewed by humans, exactly as in 2015. To add AI would require rebuilding their data pipeline and retraining their QA team — a 12–18 month project.

No API — enterprise customers cannot integrate programmatically. Adding an API would require architectural changes to a legacy platform that likely wasn't designed for it.

Revenue declining — they cannot fund a rebuild from declining cash flow.

Mosaic's Competitive Window

BeMyEye's customers are actively evaluating alternatives. Their enterprise contracts typically renew annually — each renewal cycle is a sales opportunity for Mosaic.

Target customer list: every major FMCG brand in EU that references BeMyEye as a "field intelligence partner" in any press release is a warm lead. Estimated 120+ enterprise accounts across EU.

Switching window: open120+ EU enterprise leads
Field Agent
Founded 2010 · Rogers, Arkansas · $22M raised · ~3M registered agents (US) · Acquired by Inmar Intelligence 2022
Limited Competitor

What They Built

The US equivalent of BeMyEye. Enterprise-only crowdsourced field intelligence with a mobile app and web portal. Acquired by Inmar Intelligence in 2022 for strategic CPG data reasons. Focused exclusively on US market after 12+ years.

Strengths: recognized brand in US CPG field intelligence, enterprise sales team, existing customer relationships with major US brands.

Why They're Not a Threat Globally

US-only with no international expansion plans after 12 years. If you're a CPG brand operating in EU, LATAM, or APAC — Field Agent is not relevant.

No AI extraction layer — same raw photo review model as BeMyEye. Post-acquisition by Inmar, innovation is unlikely to accelerate.

Enterprise-only — no consumer or SMB tier. Cannot execute the viral growth model Mosaic uses for collector acquisition.

Coexistence Strategy

Mosaic's initial markets (London, NYC, Berlin, Amsterdam) have zero overlap with Field Agent's US-centric coverage. International CPG brands operating in EU have no quality alternative.

In the US, Mosaic competes on AI extraction quality, API access, and price. Field Agent's post-acquisition innovation trajectory is unclear — but their legacy tech makes a rapid product response unlikely.

US: price + AI + API advantageEU: uncontested
Trax
Founded 2010 · Singapore · $750M+ raised · ~$100M ARR · Real-time shelf analytics via in-store cameras
Hardware-Locked

What They Built

The most technically advanced competitor: permanent in-store cameras with real-time computer vision. Best-in-class accuracy for the stores that have them. $100M+ ARR, $1B+ valuation. Real technology, real customers, real AI.

Used by Coca-Cola, Nestlé, and major retailers. Their accuracy is genuinely excellent for stores with installed hardware.

The Hardware Lock Problem

$50,000+ per store for hardware installation. A CPG brand with 4,200 stores cannot afford Trax for all locations — the economics only work for the top 50–100 priority stores.

This creates an inherent 95%+ coverage gap. Trax customers still need Mosaic (or similar) for the stores without hardware. We're complements, not pure substitutes, for the high end.

Why Trax Won't Disrupt Down

Trax's entire business model, investor story, and sales team is built around hardware. Pivoting to a crowdsourced, no-hardware model would cannibalize their core revenue and require rebuilding their go-to-market from scratch.

Additionally, Trax's accuracy advantage disappears in crowdsourced contexts — the structured camera environment is what makes their vision models work. Open-world photos require different AI architecture.

Complement for <100 storesNo down-market pivot

GDPR Compliance Architecture

Physical world data collection has a clear GDPR path — we designed for it from day zero:

  • Collector consent — explicit GDPR-compliant data processing consent at onboarding, with clear task data retention policies
  • No personal data in outputs — AI explicitly trained to detect and exclude human faces and vehicle plates before delivery to enterprise customers
  • Data residency — EU collector data stored on EU-region infrastructure (AWS Frankfurt), no transatlantic transfer of personal data
  • Right to erasure — automated collector data deletion pipeline within 30 days of account closure
  • DPA with enterprise customers — standard Data Processing Agreement included in all enterprise contracts

Why the Stack Works

Every vertical uses the same three assets: the collector network, the AI extraction engine, and the proof-of-completion infrastructure. Adding insurance doesn't require rebuilding anything — it requires defining new task types and adding property-specific AI extractors.

The collector network is the scarce resource. Collectors commuting past grocery stores are also commuting past properties, logistics hubs, and government infrastructure. They don't need to be re-acquired to serve the next vertical.

The CPG beachhead is not the product. The CPG beachhead is the fastest path to building the collector network and proprietary training data that powers every vertical. We're playing the long game from day one.
