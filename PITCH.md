## Slide 1: The Problem

**Headline**: The physical world is dark.

Businesses make billion-dollar decisions based on data that's weeks old, survey-based, and covers less than 10% of actual locations.

**Three specific pains** (each with a number):

1. **CPG brands** lose 4–8% of revenue to out-of-stocks they can never see. For a $500M brand, that's $20–40M/year in invisible losses.

2. **Mystery shopping** — the incumbent solution — takes 2–3 weeks and costs $50–$300 per location. By the time you know about a competitor price cut, you've already lost the weekend.

3. **Field sales teams** cover fewer than 5% of store locations. The other 95% are flying blind.

The physical world generates no data unless someone deliberately captures it. That has to change.

---

## Slide 2: Why Now

Three forces have converged that make this possible in 2026 — impossible before 2022:

1. **Ubiquitous smartphones**: 6.8B smartphone users worldwide. Any of them can be an intelligence agent. The sensor network already exists.

2. **On-device AI**: MediaPipe and custom TFLite models run face detection and quality validation in under 15ms on any mid-range phone. Privacy protection can now happen before a single pixel leaves the device.

3. **Gig economy at scale**: 50M+ gig workers globally comfortable earning from micro-tasks. They're already driving, delivering, assembling. Adding "photograph" to that menu is obvious.

The combination of these three forces creates a new category that did not exist before: crowdsourced, AI-validated physical world intelligence.

---

## Slide 3: The Solution

**Headline**: Mosaic — Physical World Intelligence

**How it works** (4 steps):

1. **Enterprise posts a task**: "Photograph shelf 4, aisle 7, Walmart #1234, Chicago. Capture all energy drink SKUs and price tags."

2. **Agent accepts + captures**: Nearby agent opens app, accepts the task, captures photos. Real-time face redaction happens on-device — no faces ever transmitted.

3. **AI validates + extracts**: Our pipeline validates authenticity (GPS, timestamp, cryptographic proof), then extracts structured data: SKU list, prices, shelf share %, out-of-stock flags.

4. **Customer sees the world**: Structured JSON delivered in 2–24 hours. Dashboard updated. Salesforce record updated. The physical world is now readable.

## Appendix A: Agent Safety + Privacy Deep Dive

**Q: What if an agent photographs people against their will?**

Technical answer: On-device face detection fires on every preview frame. Any detected face is automatically blurred before the frame is encoded. No face data ever leaves the device. Server-side secondary check confirms no faces in upload. Submissions with faces: rejected, agent warned.

Legal answer: The mystery shopper framework means agents operate as standard retail customers in public spaces — a practice with 50+ years of legal precedent. Tasks are never designed to photograph individuals as subjects.

**Q: What about GDPR?**

Face redaction before transmission means no personal data (as defined by GDPR Article 4) is ever processed by Pixie's servers. We are not a data controller for personal data of individuals captured during tasks — because no such data is ever transmitted. This is a technical compliance position, not just a policy one.
