# MOSAIC — Presenter Notes (Slide by Slide)
**What to say. What NOT to say. What questions to anticipate.**

---

## Slide 1 — Cover
**SAY:** "Mosaic. Physical world intelligence, on demand. We turn any smartphone into a shelf intelligence device — at 15× the speed and 4× cheaper than anyone in the market."

**DON'T SAY:** "We're a marketplace" or "we're like Uber for X." Say: *intelligence platform*.

**ANTICIPATE:** "What is this exactly?" → Have the demo ready. Say: "Let me show you — 2 minutes."

---

## Slide 2 — Problem
**SAY:** Lead with the $1.1T number. "A trillion dollars in out-of-stock losses annually — not because brands don't care, but because they literally cannot see inside stores fast enough to do anything about it."

**Key stat to hit hard:** "A brand manager with 50,000 stores visits 2,400 of them per year. They're flying blind on 95% of their distribution."

**DON'T SAY:** "Traditional companies are slow." Too generic. Say: "The technology to solve this didn't exist before 2024."

**ANTICIPATE:** "Is this really a $35B market?" → "Yes — that's retail intelligence + mystery shopping + insurance field services + AI training data. We're not making it up: IRI, NielsenIQ, and BeMyEye all exist in this market."

---

## Slide 3 — Solution
**SAY:** Walk the flow quickly. "Enterprise posts a task in 90 seconds. Mosaic routes it to agents already near those stores. Photos taken, AI extracts structured data. Results delivered in 4 hours." Pause. "Not 3 weeks. 4 hours."

**DON'T SAY:** "We use GPT-4V" — say "multimodal AI vision models" unless they ask. VCs hear GPT-4V as OpenAI dependency risk.

**ANTICIPATE:** "What's your AI accuracy?" → "90%+ on structured shelf data extraction. We validate every result with a confidence score — low-confidence tasks trigger a re-capture request."

---

## Slide 4 — Why Now
**SAY:** Spend 30 seconds on MediaPipe on-device — this is the technical barrier competitors can't cross. "Face redaction on-device via MediaPipe and TFLite. GDPR-native. This wasn't reliably possible before 2024."

**DON'T SAY:** "Now is the right time" generically. Name the specific inflection: the convergence of on-device AI, gig maturity, and multimodal extraction quality.

**ANTICIPATE:** "Why not earlier?" → "Earlier, you couldn't run face redaction on a 2020 smartphone without burning the battery and crashing the app. GPT-4V class accuracy on shelf photos wasn't there until 2023. Gig workers weren't yet comfortable with micro-task apps until Deliveroo/Uber had normalized it."

---

## Slide 5 — Product
**SAY:** Focus on Commute Mode. "This is the product insight that unlocks supply-side economics. Gig workers aren't making a special trip — they're on their commute. We intercept existing movement. LTV:CAC ratio: 90:1."

**DON'T SAY:** "We're building for everyone." Sharpen to: "First vertical: CPG shelf audits. Second: brand compliance. Third: wherever brands need eyes."

**ANTICIPATE:** "Can you show me the actual app?" → "Yes — the demo is live, you can interact with it."

---

## Slide 6 — Market
**SAY:** The training data number always lands. "$19.6B training data market by 2033. Every task we complete generates a proprietary labeled image. That's a second revenue line with 95% gross margin that no competitor can access at consumer scale."

**DON'T SAY:** "We can capture X% of the market." VCs hate top-down market math. Say: "We've built it bottom-up from task economics."

**ANTICIPATE:** "Your TAM is too broad." → "Fair. Our SAM is $8.5B — crowdsourced, on-demand, visual intelligence. We don't need the full $35B to be a great business. $350M SOM in year 3 makes us a Series A story."

---

## Slide 7 — Business Model
**SAY:** Explain the three tiers and why the Training Data tier is the secret weapon. "Three commercial tiers fund growth. Training data is the compounding asset. At Month 36, it could be our largest revenue line at 95% gross margin."

**DON'T SAY:** "We'll monetize the data later" — we have a concrete model. Say: "$0.10–$0.15 per labeled image, current market rate. At 100K tasks/month, that's $15–20K MRR with no additional cost."

**ANTICIPATE:** "What's your churn assumption?" → "Enterprise: 2.5%/month (12x industry best practice). SMB: 5%. Consumer: 15%. These are conservative — enterprise customers with annual contracts churn at 1.5% in comparable platforms."

---

## Slide 8 — Traction
**SAY:** Lead with what's been built. "Two people, 8 weeks, $30K/month. Agent app. Enterprise portal. AI pipeline. Route-matching. GDPR architecture. That's the AI-native founding advantage."

**DON'T SAY:** Apologize for being pre-revenue. Reframe: "Pre-launch is the right time to raise. The product works. We're raising to deploy, not to build."

**ANTICIPATE:** "Why haven't you launched yet?" → "We built the full product before raising so investors see what they're actually funding. Too many seed decks show wireframes. This is real."

---

## Slide 9 — Go-to-Market
**SAY:** Hit the Beachhead → Expansion → Category Default narrative. "We're not trying to boil the ocean. Three cities in 6 months. Prove unit economics. Then series A and 8+ cities."

**KEY POINT:** "One EU enterprise anchor — one Unilever, one Heineken — opens every enterprise door in Europe. That's the pattern in B2B SaaS: win the anchor, get the category."

**DON'T SAY:** "We'll do social media marketing." Say: "Supply side: TikTok/Instagram for commuter demographics. Enterprise: founder-led outreach at Month 2."

**ANTICIPATE:** "How do you get agents in a new city?" → "City launch playbook: 500 agents before we open to enterprise. Targeted social campaigns targeting commuters. $8–12 CPA. We've modeled this."

---

## Slide 10 — Competition
**SAY:** Point to the white space in the matrix. "Notice where Mosaic sits — high AI quality, consumer + SMB accessible. Nobody else is here. That's not an accident. BeMyEye made a business decision never to serve this tier. We're building for the era of self-serve intelligence."

**DON'T SAY:** "Our competitors are bad companies." Say: "BeMyEye reached $35M in 15 years doing it their way. We'll do it faster because we're not limited by their legacy approach."

**ANTICIPATE:** "Can Field Agent add AI?" → "They could build it over 12–18 months. Meanwhile we build supply density and sign enterprise contracts. First-mover with supply-side lock-in wins."

---

## Slide 11 — Team
**SAY:** Own the "two-person team" narrative. "We're not under-resourced — we're AI-native. Claude Code, Cursor, v0, Supabase. Traditional company has 8 engineers and 2 PMs. We have 2 founders who ship faster because there's no coordination overhead."

**DON'T SAY:** "We'll hire X people with the funding." Say what you'll build with those hires specifically.

**ANTICIPATE:** "What happens if one of you leaves?" → Standard co-founder vesting with 1-year cliff. Address directly: "We've worked together before, we have alignment on vision and equity split."

---

## Slide 12 — Financials
**SAY:** The bar chart makes the trajectory obvious. "Month 6 to Month 18 is the critical phase — $99K to $822K MRR. That's the 3-city prove-out. By Month 24 we're at $1.6M MRR. Break-even at Month 20."

**KEY ASSUMPTION to highlight:** "40% agent payout is locked — it's in our agent terms of service. It's a trust signal. We're not going to pull an Uber and cut rates at scale."

**DON'T SAY:** "These are conservative." Every VC hears that. Say: "These are built bottom-up from task economics. Here's the math per task."

**ANTICIPATE:** "How do you get to 50 enterprise customers in 18 months?" → "Founder-led outreach. Month 1: 5 pilot customers at $0 to prove the workflow. Month 6: 10 paying at $2.5K/month. Month 18: 50 at blended $5K/month. BeMyEye has 300+ enterprise customers — we need 50."

---

## Slide 13 — The Ask
**SAY:** Make the FOMO explicit. "BeMyEye hasn't shipped a meaningful product update since 2020. US gig platforms haven't noticed Europe. Enterprise AI adoption is 3× faster than 2023. The window is real. We've built the product. $5M closes the round."

**SAY:** Be specific about what $5M buys. "3 engineers. 1 AE. Agent acquisition in 4 cities. Legal for GDPR and SOC 2. 18 months to Series A metrics."

**DON'T SAY:** "We're talking to other investors" as a pressure tactic. VCs see through it. Instead: "We're targeting a 60-day close. We're selective — we want a fund that's seen this type of marketplace-meets-intelligence play before."

**ANTICIPATE:** "What's your preferred investor profile?" → "Marketplace experience, B2B SaaS background, EU familiarity a plus. Bessemer, Point Nine, Local Globe — they've seen this pattern. We'd love an intro if you know them."

**CLOSE:** Don't pitch past the yes. If they lean in, say: "Can we set up a follow-up to walk through the financial model and data room?"

---

## General Rules
- **Say the $5M before they ask.** Don't wait for "so how much are you raising?"
- **Never say "we're pre-revenue, but..."** — reframe as "we built first."
- **Lead with the demo when in doubt.** The product does the selling.
- **Time your slides:** 3 min for problem+solution, 2 min for why now + product, 1 min per remaining slide, 5 min Q&A.
