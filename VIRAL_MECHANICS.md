# Pixie — Viral Mechanics

## Overview

Pixie needs virality on both sides simultaneously:
- **Supply virality**: Agents recruit agents (lower CAC, builds density fast)
- **Demand virality**: Enterprise customers refer enterprise customers (faster sales cycle, warmer leads)

Neither side is traditionally "viral" in the consumer sense — enterprise buyers don't post TikToks. But both sides have natural word-of-mouth loops that can be accelerated.

---

## Supply-Side Virality: Agent Growth Loops

### Loop 1: Earnings Social Share

**Trigger**: Agent receives payment confirmation
**Mechanic**: App shows earnings card with one-tap share to Instagram/TikTok/WhatsApp
**Content**: "Made $47 in 2 hours photographing [City] today ⚡" (no Pixie logo overload — just the number and city)
**Why it works**: Gig workers trust peer income proof over any advertisement. "My friend made $47 in 2 hours" converts at 10x a banner ad.
**Virality coefficient target**: Each agent share generates 0.3 new agent installs

### Loop 2: Agent-to-Agent Referral

**Mechanic**: Agent A earns $25 when referred Agent B completes 10 tasks
**Agent B earns**: $15 welcome bonus on first task completion
**Sharing trigger**: Referral code visible on earnings screen + "Share and earn $25" CTA
**Network effect**: Gig worker friend groups tend to cluster. One Uber driver who joins and earns well tells 5 driver friends. Those 5 become agents, tell their networks.
**Target referral rate**: 1 in 5 agents refers at least one new agent

### Loop 3: City Leaderboard

**Mechanic**: Weekly leaderboard showing top 10 earners per city
**Public vs private**: Top earners get opt-in public profile ("Dallas's top Scout this week earned $890")
**Motivator**: Competitive agents compete to climb leaderboard. Visible earnings data at top of leaderboard recruits new agents.
**WhatsApp/Telegram groups**: City Captains seed city-level agent chat groups. Leaderboard results shared. "Top agent in Atlanta made $890 this week — join now."

### Loop 4: Campus Ambassador Cascade

**Mechanic**: Campus ambassador recruits 10+ agents → earns $200/month credit → becomes local success story → recruits more ambassadors
**Cascade**: 1 Ambassador → 10–50 agents (direct recruits) → some of those become sub-ambassadors
**Events**: Ambassadors host "Pixie earn sessions" on campus (demo the app, show earnings potential, help first-timers complete their first task)
**Target**: Each ambassador generates 3x their direct recruits through their network's network

## Supply-Side: City Siege Event

The most powerful agent growth mechanic. Runs monthly in each active city.

### Format
- Duration: 48 hours (Friday 6pm → Sunday 6pm)
- Agent incentive: 2x base rate on all tasks during window
- Special tasks: "City Siege Challenges" — capture 5 specific locations, earn $50 bonus
- Leaderboard: Real-time leaderboard updated every 30 minutes during event
- Top 10 agents: $100–$500 bonus prizes

### Customer Value
- City Siege demonstrates platform scale to prospective customers: "In 48 hours, our Atlanta network captured 3,000 shelf audits across the city"
- Sales team books demos for the week after each City Siege, showing the data collected as a live product demo
- Press opportunity: "Local photographers earn $X in weekend data sprint"

### Viral Mechanics During City Siege
- Agents share real-time earnings on social media (leaderboard creates FOMO)
- Top agent at the end of the weekend: feature story on Pixie blog + social media
- "I made $340 in one weekend" content is inherently shareable
- New agent surge: City Siege drives 30–50% spike in new agent sign-ups the following week

### Budget per City Siege
- Agent rate premium (2x): ~$20K per city (1,000 tasks × $20 incremental payout)
- Prize pool: $5,000
- Marketing (social ads, local press outreach): $3,000
- Total: ~$28,000/city/month
- ROI: 30–50 new agents × $25 CAC saved = $750–$1,250 in CAC savings + 1,000 tasks of data for sales demos

## Demand-Side Virality: Customer Growth Loops

### Loop 1: The CPG Network Effect

CPG Brand Managers are a tight professional community. They:
- Meet at the same 5 industry conferences per year
- Are members of the same LinkedIn groups
- Move between competing companies (today's Coca-Cola brand manager is tomorrow's PepsiCo brand manager)
- Actively compare notes on tools and data sources

**Mechanic**: Happy customer shares insight ("We caught a competitor price cut across 200 stores in 4 hours — Pixie is the tool")
**Virality trigger**: Build a shareable "Pixie Insights Report" — a one-page PDF snapshot the customer can forward to a colleague or present at a conference
**Referral incentive**: Customer earns $1,000 credit per referral that pays >$5K/month
**Target**: Every 4 paying customers generates 1 new customer via referral

### Loop 2: Conference Live Demo

**Tactic**: At Expo West or GroceryShop, set up a live shelf scan in an exhibitor's booth.
- Demonstrate: "We're going to capture competitive shelf data from 10 stores within 1 mile of this venue — in the next 30 minutes."
- During the demo: tasks are being completed by pre-positioned agents (or organic agents who happen to be nearby)
- Show results live: map view with completion pins populating in real-time, AI extraction results appearing
- Reaction: conference attendees watch physical world intelligence happen in real-time

This demo is the most powerful sales tool Pixie has. Abstract pitch decks sell nothing. Watching your competitors' prices update live on a dashboard sells contracts.

### Loop 3: Market Research Agency Integration

**Target partners**: IRI (now Circana), Kantar, Nielsen, SPINS (natural food focus), Numerator

**Pitch**: "Add Pixie's on-demand physical shelf data to your syndicated reports. You give clients the same data they already trust, plus real-time physical ground truth they can't get anywhere else."

**Economics**: Agency adds 20–30% markup to Pixie's data in their reports. Pixie earns wholesale rate, agency earns margin. Agency manages customer relationship.

**Why agencies love it**: Differentiated data (they can't get it elsewhere), no tech build required, adds value to existing client contracts.

**Scale**: One partnership with a firm like Circana = access to 2,000+ CPG clients at no incremental CAC

### Loop 4: Data-as-Press

**Mechanic**: Pixie publishes monthly "Physical World Intelligence Reports":
- "US Retail Out-of-Stock Index: March 2026"
- "Energy Drink Shelf Share: Q1 2026 Analysis"
- "Where Nike vs Adidas is Winning at Retail"

**Distribution**: LinkedIn (CPG audience), Axios Pro Retail, Food Dive, Progressive Grocer, CNBC

**Outcome**: Media coverage drives inbound enterprise leads. Brand managers read "Nike vs Adidas" and wonder: "Can I get this for my brand?"

**Cost**: 2 hours of analyst time to compile from existing data. Zero additional cost. Maximum impact.

## Flywheel Map

```
More agents
    ↓
Higher fulfillment rate
    ↓
More enterprise customers acquired
    ↓
More tasks posted
    ↓
More agent earnings → More agents recruited
    ↓
More AI training data produced
    ↓
Better AI extraction → More customer value
    ↓
Higher ACV / expansion revenue
    ↓
More enterprise customers acquired...
```

The flywheel has no external dependency after initial seeding. It's self-sustaining above a density threshold.

**Critical threshold**: ~500 agents per city and ~3 paying customers per city. Below this, the flywheel stalls. Above this, it accelerates.

## Viral Coefficient Targets

| Mechanic | K-factor Target | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Agent earnings social share | 0.3 per active agent/month | 30% share → 30% of shares generate install |
| Agent referral program | 0.2 per active agent/month | 1 in 5 agents refers 1+ friend |
| City Siege event | 0.5 spike/event/city | 50 new agents per event beyond normal rate |
| CPG customer referral | 0.25 per paying customer | 1 referral per 4 customers per month |
| Market research agency | 2.0 per partnership | Each agency partnership generates 2+ direct customers |

**Combined viral coefficient**: ~0.5+ (platform doesn't go viral on its own, but significantly lowers effective CAC — each paid acquisition generates 0.5 additional organic acquisitions)

**Goal**: Effective CAC = paid CAC × 0.67 (i.e., 33% of growth comes from organic/viral)
